Luke Bryan, Jason Aldean + More Head Up 2019 Stagecoach Festival

February 2024 · 2 minute read

Luke Bryan, Sam Hunt and Jason Aldean have been announced as the headliners for the 2019 Stagecoach Festival, which is set to take place in California from April 26-28.

The festival is one of the biggest in country music each year, and it returns to its longtime home at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, Calif., in its 13th year. Bryan is set to headline Friday night (April 26), while Hunt tops the lineup on Saturday (April 27). Three-time 2018 CMA nominee Aldean will take the stage on Sunday night (April 28) to close out the Stagecoach Festival in 2019.

Cole Swindell, Luke Combs, Old Dominion, Kane Brown, Cam, Lauren Alaina, Lynyrd Skynyrd, Bret Michaels, Tom Jones and more are also set for the three-day festival, which will also feature Guy Fieri’s Stagecoach Smokehouse, the SiriusXM Spotlight Stage, Nikki Lane's Stage Stop Marketplace, the Honkytonk Dance Hall and more.

Tickets for the 2019 Stagecoach Festival are set to go on sale Friday (Oct. 19) at 11AM PDT at the festival's website. Price range from $349 for a three-day general admission pass to $899 for three-day Corral C2 reserved seating. 2019 will also see the return of car camping, with the option to purchase companion camping parking for additional vehicles. Amenities including the internet cafe, charging stations, general store, supermarket shuttles and on-site pedal cabs will also be available for purchase, along with special travel packages, shuttles and the new Safari Camping Experience, through Valley Music Travel.
