Early Life

Gina Joy Carano was born on April 16, 1982, in Dallas, Texas.

Athletic Background

Gina Carano grew up with a love for sports and excelled in various disciplines, including basketball, volleyball, and softball.

Kickboxing Champion

Before transitioning into mixed martial arts, Gina Carano achieved great success as a professional Muay Thai fighter, amassing a record of 12 wins and 1 loss.

MMA Pioneer

Gina Carano played a pivotal role in popularizing women’s MMA, becoming one of the sport’s most recognizable figures.

Movie Debut

Gina Carano made her feature film debut in the 2009 action-thriller “Blood and Bone,” alongside Michael Jai White.

“Haywire” Breakthrough

Gina Carano gained widespread critical acclaim for her lead role in Steven Soderbergh’s 2011 film “Haywire,” where she showcased her impressive fighting skills.

Fast & Furious Franchise

Carano joined the cast of “Fast & Furious 6” in 2013, portraying the fierce DSS agent Riley Hicks.

“Deadpool” Connection

In the 2016 superhero film “Deadpool,” Gina Carano starred as the mutant character Angel Dust, marking her collaboration with Ryan Reynolds.

American Gladiators

Carano served as a Gladiator on the revival of the popular TV show “American Gladiators” in 2008, competing under the name “Crush.”
