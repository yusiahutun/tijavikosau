Kwon Alexander dedicates Bucs win over Falcons to slain brother Broderick Taylor

July 2024 · 4 minute read

NFL rookie Kwon Alexander had the best game of his career and led his team to an upset win on Sunday with a performance he dedicated to his brother, 17, who was shot dead on Friday night.

Alexander, 21, led the Tampa Bay Buccaneers to a 23-20 overtime victory over the Atlanta Falcons, less than 48 hours after Broderick Taylor was killed during a fight in their hometown in Alabama. 

Taylor was shot in the chest and died following an altercation in Anniston and Jerald Bailey, 24, was charged with murder after he turned himself in to police early on Saturday morning.

NFL rookie Kwon Alexander, 21, prayed before his game on Sunday. His brother was killed on Friday night

Alexander had an interception and a fumble recovery that both eventually led to scores in the win and also led his team while simultaneously setting a new career high by recording 11 tackles.

'It has been hard losing my little brother,' said Alexander, a first-year linebacker. 

'It was the only thing on my mind because the only thing that he wanted was for me to ball. 

Broderick Taylor, 17, was shot and killed on Friday 

'I know that he would want me to be strong for him, so I came out here and dedicated this game to him.

'I know he’s looking up there and he’s happy and he’s smiling.  

'I'm just ready to be back with my family.' 

The fourth-round pick out of LSU actually picked off two passes, returning the first of them 93 yards out of his own end zone before he was finally run down by Falcons star receiver Julio Jones.

That play didn't count as it was overturned because the Buccaneers were offside.

Alexander was just getting warmed up.

Before the first quarter was done, he stripped the ball from Jones after a reception over the middle, and returned it 20 yards to set up a field goal. 

Then, late in the first half, Alexander intercepted another pass from Ryan, returning it 15 yards to midfield. 

After he was tackled, he pointed toward the roof of the Georgia Dome.

The play set up a touchdown that gave the Bucs a 13-3 halftime lead.

As Alexander left the field at the Georgia Dome, his emotions caught up with him and he broke down in tears 

Alexander stripped the ball from Julio Jones (right) and returned it 20 yards to set up a first quarter field goal

'I never give up,' Alexander said, again remembering his brother.

 'He wouldn't want me to give up. It was all for him.'

Alexander could have sat the game out and no one would have blamed him, but instead he decided to take the field.

'Sometimes when you are going through tough times like that, you need to be around your other family, doing something that you really love, to take your mind off of what's going on at home,' Tampa Bay head coach Lovie Smith said.

'Losing a 17-year-old brother, I can only imagine what the family is going through. 

'I'm just glad his teammates were there for him to provide support as much as anything. 

'I left it up to him as to what he wanted to do.'

The young middle linebacker (center) could have sat the game out and no one would have blamed him

A Tampa Bay coach (left) gave Alexander a pat on the helmet after his interception in the first on Sunday 

Quarterback Jameis Winston figured his fellow rookie would have a big game.

He could tell how much Alexander wanted it.

'When you talk about the true definition of a man, it's the way he overcomes adversity and the way that he took the field,' Winston said. 

'We saw in his eyes that he was here for a purpose.'

As Alexander left the field, his emotions finally caught up with him and he broke down in tears.

He was ready to get back to his family.

'When you're doing something for a purpose, family is a strong thing,' Winston said. 

'When something happens to your family and you're a true man and you go out and represent for your family like that, that just says a lot. 

'Everybody in that locker room has tremendous respect for the man that he is.

'For him to be a rookie is even more amazing,' the quarterback added. 'That's my brother.'
