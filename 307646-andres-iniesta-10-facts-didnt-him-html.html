Andres Iniesta: 10 facts you didn't know about him

June 2024 · 12 minute read

Andrés Iniesta Luján is a Spanish professional footballer currently playing for the Japanese side Vissel Kobe. He is mostly remembered for his jersey number '8' playing at Barcelona, where he was a captain for three seasons. Unlike many professional players who join the club through a transfer deal, Iniesta soared to the starting lineup of the elite squad through La Masia, the youth academy for Barcelona FC. After his debut in the 2004-2005 season, the star remained in the club until his departure to Japan.

According to biography of Andreas Iniesta, he began his career in football as a young boy of 8 years. Between 1994 and 1996, he played for Albacete football club, marking the beginning of his great football career. Since then, he has earned numerous awards and trophies both individually, and the teams he has represented. He was an integral part of the Barcelona elite team which emerged with two historic trebles in the years 2009 and 2015. Cumulatively, Andres Iniesta has seen the club win 34 trophies. Other than the club football, the legend has also represented the national team at different tiers including the Under-16 youth, Under-19, and Under-21 levels and eventually incorporated to the Spain national team in 2006 and played his first major international match in Euro 2008.

10 facts you didn't know about Andres Iniesta

Many people remember him for his tactical play and man of the match titles he earned during his career at Barcelona. However, there are many facts about Iniesta that his fans and the football community don't know about him. Here are ten facts you should know about the star:

1. Iniesta made 674 appearances and scored 57 goals for Barcelona

Since joining the elite team, Iniesta has been one of the most consistent players in the pitch. He started as a defensive midfielder. Due to his skills and ability of perfect balance, excellent ball control, and play agility; he changed his position to an attacking midfielder. Beyond the raw talent that saw him get identified by senior club scouts, it is Iniesta's work ethic and versatility that saw him stay in the team. Thanks to his calm and bold play, he managed to appear 674 times in for the Catalans averaging to about 40 match appearances per season. For those asking how many goals has Andres Iniesta scored for Barcelona? Well, he scored only 57 goals, most of which were highly unanticipated and culminated in being the game changers for the entire club.

2. Provided 140 assists for Barcelona in 674 appearances

Most payers are famous for the number of goals they score at the end of every competition. It is not the case for the phenomenal Iniesta. He is best known for the superb assists he provides for the world's top finishers including Messi while in Barcelona and the likes of Diego Costa in the international front. He was a critical factor in the 2-0 win over Manchester United in the 2008-09 champions league final due to his midfield artistry and ball-handling skills.

After the game, Wayne Rooney the then EPL best player couldn't hesitate but term Iniesta, the best player in the world. He was subsequently named the 2009 FIFA World Player of the Year after receiving a massive 134 votes and came near fourth in the Ballon d'Or award, after garnering 149 points. Football analysis and other world-class players have cited his clinical passes and assists. He is the biggest threat to any opponent, earning him a €200 million exit clause from the Catalans.

3. Appeared 145 times in the international competitions at Barcelona

Since his 2002 debut in Barcelona, Iniesta has appeared an additional 145 times in the international competitions including 130 times in the UEFA Champions League. Iniesta was identified by Sir Alex Ferguson as the biggest threat to the then Champions League defending champions, Manchester United.

According to Ferguson, Iniesta was a fantastic player in his ability to find passes, create space for other players, and make movements. Iniesta was a world superstar when it comes to Champions League action. Other than his agility, he was identified behind the success of Barcelona during his time as a captain. His hard work is evidenced by the number of plaudits he received in the tournament every year and the 4-Champions League trophies earned for the team.

4. Won a historic double treble with Barcelona in 2009 and 2015

Iniesta came through Barcelona's youngster academy to be one of the best and most priced players in the elite team. At the end of his career, the star was the most decorated Spanish player of all time given his awards, performances, and the honours he received on different fronts. In 2009, he steered Barcelona to win La Liga, Copa Del Rey, and UEFA Champions League. In 2015, the club made a repeat of 2009 race to win La Liga, Copa Del Rey, and UEFA Champions League.

5.The third player from Barcelona to ever receive a standing ovation

During the 2015-2016 El Classico match held at the home turf of their arch-rivals, it was all applause and celebration by the home team fans. It was after Iniesta entertained them to the best football of the season. The star joined the other two Barcelona players namely Ronaldinho in 2005 and Diego Maradona in 1983 as players who ever received a standing ovation from the rival club. His man of the match performance was exhibited by an assist and a goal that saw the visitors walk away with all three points following a resounding 4-0 win.

No one can dispute Iniesta's record of play and the greatness on the pitch. It made him one of the few midfielders who have earned a reputation at the best player table. Following the success of that season, he had no choice but yielded to the pressure from fans and signed a lifetime deal with the club. It would see him end his career at the club in a time of his choosing.

6. Won 39 trophies in his professional football career

Iniesta is one of the players with the most trophies in European football. He has featured and won in numerous Champions League finals, UEFA Super Cup, FIFA Club World Cup, Copa Del Rey, Supercopa de España, and La Liga. In total, Iniesta has won in all of these competitions multiple times as well as winning a title every year since he joined the Barcelona elite squad.

As Spain's most decorated player of all time, he has also won in numerous competitions from the 2010 FIFA World Cup, UEFA European Championship (2008 and 2012), UEFA European Under-19 Championship (2002), and UEFA European Under-17 Championship (2001). The trophies he has won in conjunction with his teammates is a testimony of the contributions he has made in the world of football.

8. Iniesta is the majority shareholder of Albacete football club

In the 2011 season, Andres invested €420,000 in Albacete, his boyhood club, in a bid to upgrade the performance and operations of the football club. Given the attachment the star has for the home-based club, it is justifiable why he has such a huge amount to aid the club. In 2013 when the club was in the verge of being relegated to the fourth tier of the league, Iniesta gave €240,000 towards covering the club's pending bills and unpaid wages for the season running. Given the dynamics surrounding the football landscape in Spain, Albacete could be on the rise to higher ranks, especially with the input of a world class player in the calibre of Iniesta.

10. Iniesta owns a winery and vineyard

Andres Iniesta may have played his last world cup and the final game for the national side, but his new venture is gaining momentum. At the current Andres Iniesta age of 34 years, he is the owner of Minuto 116. It is named after the sensational goal he scored for his country in the 2010 world cup to win his country the first and only world cup title.

The choice of name, according to the company director Mr Lazaro, is strategic in giving the brand a market edge, especially in countries where football is a popular source of entertainment. According to Bloomberg Business, the winery produced 1.2 million wine bottles in 2017, recording a 500% leap from the amount produced at the same time in 2010. But while the only source of market for the product in 2010 was Spain, most of the revenue generated currently are from abroad markets.
