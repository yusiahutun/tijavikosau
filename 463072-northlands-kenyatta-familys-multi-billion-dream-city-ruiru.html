Northlands: Inside Kenyatta Family's Multi-Billion Dream City in Ruiru

March 2024 · 3 minute read
  • The Kenyatta family is currently expanding their empire by constructing a dream city in Ruiru, Kiambu county, near Kenyatta University
  • Northlands city is being constructed as an alternative to Nairobi, which is deemed congested and no longer offers a serene business environment
  • The city would comprise residential, industrial, and commercial units expected to host over 250,000 people in an expansive over 11,000 acres of land

The Kenyatta family is considered the wealthiest family in Kenya. Their patriarch is the founding president Jomo Kenyatta and scion, President Uhuru Kenyatta.

The family has invested in virtually all sectors, including banking with shares in the NCBA Group PLC, tourism through Heritage hotels, mining, insurance, airline, education, agriculture, real estate, and energy.

Northlands city

The family is expanding their empire by constructing a dream city near Kenyatta University in Ruiru, Kiambu county.

The ambitious project is expected to cost over KSh 500 billion, according to Construction Kenya.

It is being constructed as an alternative to Nairobi as it has access to the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport.

"Many of the office buildings within the CBD are not well suited to modern business requirements, and some businesses are now considering moving out of the CBD," the strategic assessment report for the city stated.

The city would comprise residential, industrial, and commercial units expected to host over 250,000 people on over 11,000 acres of land.

"Whilst a key focus will be to meet the needs of the emerging middle class, different areas will cater for older couples, families, and young professionals. Areas of lower-cost housing will also be provided to meet the needs of low-income earners and prevent the emergence of unplanned slum areas," its strategic plan states, as reported by Business Daily.

The land currently hosts Brookside Dairy Company and Gicheha farm, managed by the first family.

Northlands city's master plan

Housing

According to the upcoming city's master plan, 3,750 acres have been set aside for building residential houses.

Of these, low-density houses (601 villas + 1,320 townhouses) will be constructed on 3,134 acres, while high-density houses will sit on 306 acres.

On the other hand, medium-density houses would cover 130 acres and comprise 670 townhouses and 368 housing units in blocks of flats.

The high-density section will have 6,980 housing units and 3,100 townhouses.

Business district

The section will sit on 390 acres and host a mall/hotel and a clubhouse.

Industrial and logistics parks

The city's architects apportioned 695 acres for an industrial park and 650 acres for a logistics park.

Brookside Dairies will take 65 acres, while 1,697 acres will serve as an open recreational space with water features on 266 acres.

Gicheha farm, wildlife conservation

A bigger portion of the land, 5,156 acres, was allocated for wildlife conservation and livestock keeping at Gicheha farm.

Ngina Kenyatta's business

In related news, away from the family business, Uhuru's daughter, Ngina, charted her path and ventured into the hospitality sector.

The beautiful woman who's married owns a posh restaurant dubbed The Green Experience in Lari, Kiambu county.

The restaurant is located in the middle of Kenyatta's family tea estate in Kiambu and offers beautiful natural scenery.
