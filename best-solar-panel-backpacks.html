FAQ

Q: Are Solar Charger Backpacks Worth It for Backpackers?

Solar energy is free, so investing in a charger is worth it. This energy is also eco-friendly and easy to harness. However, if you are going on a short day trip, you may feel it is quicker and more convenient to charge your devices from a wall outlet before heading off. Another option is to take a small power bank with you.

However, for multi-day trips out into the wilderness, solar backpacks are an excellent option. They can also charge your power bank during the day so you have a power source at night by the campfire.

You should also consider that apps like GPS maps can drain your smartphone battery more quickly, so having a solar option is well worth your consideration.

Q: How Many Liters Should My Backpack Have?

The size of the backpack is primarily related to the number of people using it and how many days the trip is. If you are a family or a group of friends sharing a backpack, it will need to have a high volume.

For personal use, a backpack for a day trip should be somewhere between 10 and 30 liters. Overnight or multi-day trips should be at least 30 and up to 50 liters. However, if you are camping and cooking, you must also consider the extra equipment. More extended expeditions into the wilderness require a bag of over 50 liters.

On the other hand, if you are a commuter or business person, a small 6- to 10-liter bag may suffice. Of course, if you want to lug a laptop or portable printer around, you need to take this into consideration, too.
