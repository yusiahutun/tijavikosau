Early life

The driver was born on 22 June 1989 (age 32) in Mooresville, North Carolina, United States. He is the son of Kerry Dale Earnhardt (father) and Rene Earnhardt (stepmother). Jeffrey’s father initially pumped gas and worked at Pizza Hut. He then transitioned to racing and became a NASCAR driver.

Jeffrey’s parents divorced when he was young. Then, his father married Rene in 1999 and welcomed their daughter Kayla Earnhardt. However, Kerry and Rene had children from previous marriages, including Kerry’s two sons (Bobby and Jeffrey), and Rene had a daughter named Blade.

Jerry has a blood brother called Bobby Earnhardt and two step-siblings, Kayla and Blade. He grew up in Mooresville and attended Mooresville High School. But unfortunately, he did not go to college. Instead, he chose to become a race car driver at 18.

Career beginnings

Before he went pro, Jeffrey started as an amateur racer at 18. His first race was in the hornet division at Wythe Raceway in Rural Retreat, Virginia. Jeffrey performed well and scored three feature wins. Furthermore, he finished in the top-five in division points and received the crown of rookie of the year.

NASCAR

Jeffrey first had the NASCAR experience in 2007. He drove the number one Chevrolet for the Andy Santerre Motorsports team in the NASCAR Busch East Series. Although he did not finish at pole positions, he placed fifth in point standings but won the Most Popular Driver Award after the season ended.

In 2010, Jeffrey participated in many races that qualified him to drive on all NASCAR tracks in the upcoming 2011 season. As a result, he signed a contract with RWR to drive for the team for the entire 2011 season.

Instead of driving for RWR, he offered his driving services to other truck teams, forcing RWR to release him. Although the parties later reconciled, Jeffrey finished 12th when he participated in the 24 Hours of Daytona race.
