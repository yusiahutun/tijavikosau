Conner Ivess New Collection is Narrated by AI Carrie Bradshaw

July 2024 · 4 minute read

After presenting two IRL runway collections in a row over the last London Fashion Weeks, this season the young London-based label Conner Ives steps back into the digital realm.

“The original plan was to do one collection a year – I wanted to see how much we could slow ‘the machine’ down,” says the brand's namesake founder ahead of the SS24 launch. “But I think, on reflection, it’s important to deliver something to keep creative momentum going. Also, I love making clothes! It’s just about looking at how to do that in keeping with our ethos.”

Ives is from Bedford, New York but now based in the U.K.—he graduated from Central Saint Martins three years ago. He's known for working as sustainably as possible, employing deadstock and upcycled clothes and materials to create new garments. (It’s worth noting that since its inception in 2021, the brand has already repurposed nearly 15,000 T-shirts that would have ended up in textile scrap yards).

It’s clear that Ives really does love exploring the playful side of fashion, showing that getting dressed can—and should be—fun and glamorous, without skimping on integrity. Using quintessentially American character archetypes, both real and imagined, Ives creates clothes based on their qualities.

Courtesy Connor Ives

This time around, model of the moment Alex Consani (who closed Ives’ AW23 show as ‘The Bride’) stars in a lookbook and campaign video captured by Johnny Dufort and styled by his long time collaborator Vanessa Reid. The latter is narrated by a rather dystopian AI rendered voiceover of ‘Carrie Bradshaw’.

The collection itself is titled ‘Late Capitalism’, which Ives explains is a tongue in cheek observation on the times we live in. “The inspiration behind the collection started with a TikTok I saw of a girl in New York waiting in a long line for a sample sale at Dover Street Market,” he says. “It was during the time that the Canadian wildfires turned the sky in the city orange. An impending ecological crisis serving as the backdrop to one of the industry’s many ‘not-to-miss’ consumerist moments, seemed to sum up the cultural mood.”

The archetypes assumed by Consani are said to represent various players on the late capitalist stage. “So there’s ‘The Girl Scout’, a budding new member of our bear market who’s competing to sell as many cookies as she can; ‘The Camp Counselor’, who wears pieces with signatures of our studio interns written in sharpies on it; and ‘The Depop Girl’, who is obsessed with all things Y2K and wears a reworked T-shirt that says ‘Spoiled, Pampered and Adored’, which I just love.”

Courtesy Connor Ives

In fact, in a very symbiotic partnership, Conner Ives has joined forces with Depop to source its deadstock and raw materials for SS24. This will extend into production and feature an exclusive capsule collection launching in November. “In pieces such as a bias cut evening dress and skirt, we’ve reworked deadstock rolls of Air Tex and lace, to create sheer layering. Throughout the collection, we’ve also used end of life Swarovski crystals that Swarovski would have been getting rid of, and recycled spandex throughout.” Ives has even gone so far as to create a strapless Paco Rabanne-esque gown and shoulder bag out of soda can tabs. “It felt so us,” he says of the pieces. “It was a very familiar American object being transformed into something that feels so foreign from where it started off.”

Courtesy Connor Ives

The brand has also worked with Jimmy Choo to reissue pieces from its archive that Ives found whilst scouring second hand stores: a boot, a mule and a slipper constructed from surplus stock leather. “I met with Jimmy Choo’s creative director Sandra Choi and she was totally aligned with what we wanted to do. We didn’t know what would be possible, but she was so on board in recreating these shapes from the late 90s. It’s our way of saying that beautiful things don’t have to be forgotten entirely.”

If this is what Ives' vision for the end of the world looks like, then I’m more than ready for the sartorial rapture.
