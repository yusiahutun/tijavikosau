Almost gone. Matt Rife tickets to his 2023-2024 comedy tour are almost out. The comedian announced his comedy tour, dubbed the ProbleMATTic Tour, on June 5, 2023, and fans moved at lightning speed.

Rife built up his comedy career in the past decade by touring and appearing on shows like Wild N’ Out, where he was the youngest cast member to ever appear on the show. However, he got his big break on TikTok where he has amassed over 13 million followers and more than 2 billion views globally. The comedian announced his tour on Youtube with the help of celebrity couple Ashton Kutcher and Mila Kunis.

“I couldn’t be more thrilled to live out this lifelong dream! To tour the world, spreading laughter, with some of my best friends, is something I never thought would be possible. I’m so grateful to get this opportunity, and it’s even more fulfilling knowing that it’s all because of the incredible people who enjoy my comedy,” the TikTok comedian said in a press release. “I’ve been working so hard towards this for 12 years and now I’ve surrounded myself with a team who works just as hard and share the same passion! I still can’t believe it.”

If you’re a fan of his crowd work content, tough news is that he won’t do it that often. “I don’t do a lot of crowd work, he told the Los Angeles Daily News. “I only maybe do five to 10 minutes of it a show, and that’s after I’ve done an hourlong set. I never plan to do crowd work, and I always emphasize that if you’re coming to my show exclusively to see that, don’t come. I’m not a jukebox. You can’t expect a certain song for me to come and play. But, If you’re patient, good, and fun, it’ll probably happen naturally.”

The tour kicked off on Thursday, July 20, 2023 in Bend, Oregon at Hayden Homes Amphitheater with stops across North America, Australia and Europe. Rife released his first comedy special “Only Fans” and self-released his highly anticipated second stand-up special, “Matthew Steven Rife” on YouTube in April 2023. He will be taping his third comedy special in Washington DC.
