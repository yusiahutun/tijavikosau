Kenyan Shilling Continues to Strengthen, Exchanges at 127 Against US Dollar

March 2024 · 3 minute read
  • The Kenyan shilling is on an upward trajectory against the United States (US) dollar and other currencies, including the Ugandan shilling
  • Following the buyback of the Eurobond loan due in June of this year, the Kenyan shilling appreciated against the dollar
  • FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau noted a stronger local currency would deal a blow to exporters, as the cost of locally manufactured goods would soar

TUKO.co.ke journalist Japhet Ruto brings over eight years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting, offering deep insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

The value of the Kenyan shilling has significantly increased in recent weeks as it continues to record gains against the United States (US) dollar and other currencies.

On Tuesday, April 9, the local currency traded at 127 against the US dollar.

Some causes of this growing tendency include the Central Bank of Kenya's monetary policies, strong foreign exchange reserves, and increased investor confidence in the Kenyan economy.

"The Kenya shilling remained stable against major international and regional currencies during the week ending April 4," the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) stated in its weekly bulletin.

According to CBK, the usable foreign exchange reserves remained adequate at $7,148 million, meeting its statutory requirement to maintain at least four months of import cover.

Impact of a strong shilling

Importers were expected to gain from the stronger shilling, including lower import costs and price stability.

However, FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau noted a stronger local currency would deal a blow to exporters, as the cost of goods from the country would soar.

"This strengthening of the Kenya shilling is expected to weaken Kenyan exporters' business," Kamau told TUKO.co.ke.

While analysing Kenya's debt, Kamau said there was uncertainty on whether the shilling would remain strong until the debt in dollars was repaid.

"Reducing debt is an overreach. It's true that Kenya's debt on the books is reducing due to forex gains, but there's no guarantee that the forex gains will hold until repayment time. In addition, if we need, say, $200 million, we can't print that; we will have to earn it in some way regardless of the shilling's strength," he explained.

Why the shilling gained value

  • Following the buyback of the Eurobond loan due in June of this year, the Kenyan shilling appreciated against the dollar.
  • CBK governor Kamau Thugge admitted that the country has been trying to maintain an “artificially strong exchange” rate, explaining that the Kenyan currency was now finding its true value.
  • According to a recent Bloomberg report, the Kenya shilling emerged as a top-performing currency globally, surpassing many counterparts, including the British Pound,
