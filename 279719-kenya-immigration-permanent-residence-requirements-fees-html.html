Kenya immigration permanent residence requirements

There are four ways to gain permanent residency in Kenya. All these four ways have different requirements and fees. Before applying for a permanent residency position, it is important that you understand each of the ways and find out which one best suits your situation.

Category A - ex-citizens of Kenya applicants

Note that this option is reserved to all Kenyan citizens by birth but at the time of application had;

  • Denounced or revoked their Kenyan citizenship
  • Been denied dual citizenship status by their countries of domicile
  • Spouses and children of the above two applicants

Requirements

To qualify for the above permanent residency position one has to have:

  • Dully completed Form 23
  • An application letter
  • Filled in a questionnaire for aspiring applicants- Category A
  • Previous proof of Kenyan citizenship, that is, a copy of Kenyan identity card, birth certificate or a Kenyan passport
  • Two most current passport sized photos
  • Copy of their current passport
  • Police clearance certificate given by the country of domicile

Kenya immigration permanent residence fees

Other than complying with the above requirements you need to pay certain fees. Under this category, you are required to pay Kenyan shillings ten thousand (Ksh. 10,000) as processing fees which are non-refundable. Once your application is approved, you will be required to pay fifteen thousand Kenya shillings (Ksh. 15,000) for issuance. The Ksh. 15,000 is a one-time payment.

Category B ( lawful residents of Kenya) applicants with work permits for at least 7 years

This second category of immigration Kenya permanent residence applicants caters for individuals who have worked in Kenya for 7 years and have shown interest in living in Kenya. To qualify for this category, other than working for seven years, you need to have stayed in the country for three years after application. Spouses and children of such applicants are eligible for permanent residency. Before applying, ensure you can provide proof of the seven years work duration and your three years of harmonious stay in Kenya. It would also be a huge plus if you can demonstrate a positive social and economic impact in the community you have lived.

Category D - Spouses of Kenya citizens

The final provision of the Kenya immigration permanent residence is given to spouses of Kenyan citizens. To qualify under this category one needs to be married for at least three years. You must also furnish the necessary authorities with sufficient proof of marriage other than the marriage certificate and present yourself before an interviewing panel. Below are some of the necessary documentation requirements you need to have;

  • An application letter
  • A filled Form 23
  • Two most current passport sized photos
  • Dully filled Category D questionnaire for aspiring applicants
  • An original Police clearance certificate
  • Copy of a legal marriage certificate (certified)
  • Sufficient proof of family and marriage life
  • Dependent work permit/ passport if they are residing in Kenya
  • Filled residence status form
  • Two passport sized photos
  • Copies of birth certificate/ Identity card/ passport/ birth certificate of the Kenyan spouse
