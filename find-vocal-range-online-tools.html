5 Online Tools to Help You Find Your Vocal Range

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Finding your vocal range allows you to identify the notes that you can sing comfortably. Identifying your vocal range on your own might be a bit challenging, especially with no background in music.

Singing within your vocal range allows you to keep your voice in good shape by not straining it, and helps you avoid causing long-term damage to your voice. Here are five online tools and websites to find your vocal range.

1. Singing Carrots

Singing Carrots is an online tool that you can use to find your vocal range. When you visit this website, you’ll come across a prompt to start a voice warm-up before performing the vocal range test. Doing a warm-up before singing allows you to keep your voice healthy and could help you to identify your vocal range more accurately.

If you’ve already warmed up your voice, you can ignore the prompt and start the vocal range test. Here, you’ll need to ensure your device's mic is on and has permission to work on this website.

To find your current pitch, you need to sing using the ‘laaa’ sound and hold the note for a few seconds. Then, you can sing a few more notes, including the highest and lowest notes you can reach. Once you’re done, the vocal range test will provide your results.

If you want to expand your vocal range, Singing Carrots also provides pitch training to help you do just that.

2. Ramsey Voice Studio

If you’re looking for a tool that’s quick and easy to use, this is it. Ramsey Voice Studio has a simple user interface that allows you to find your vocal range in three steps.

To use this tool, the first step is to click the mic and sing your lowest note, then the website will display your lowest note on the screen.

The second step is to click the other mic and start singing your highest note. After singing your highest note, you’ll have to enter your email address to receive your results, then you’re done.

3. ToneGym

ToneGym is an online platform that provides ear training and music theory in a fun and effective way. On this website, you’ll find loads of music information and tools to help you grasp multiple components of music. One of the tools included is a vocal range test.

ToneGym’s vocal range test doesn’t only help you identify how wide your vocal range is, but it also helps you find out what your voice type is and how your range compares to famous singers. That way, even when you’re singing karaoke songs, you can enjoy songs of artists that share a similar vocal range.

4. My Vocal Range

This website requires you to do some manual work in order to find out what your vocal range is. To get started, you’ll need to find your lowest note by playing the numbered piano notes on the website and sing along using the ‘aaah’ sound.

Once you’ve found your lowest note, you can follow the same process to find your highest note. Make sure you note down your highest and lowest note, that way, you can refer back to it whenever you need to.

5. Vocal Nebula

Vocal Nebula is a platform that aims to help you train your voice and improve your singing through free and premium courses, as well as a collection of tools. One of the tools you’ll find on this website is a vocal range and voice type test.

The vocal range and voice type test provides you with an instructional guide to help you identify your vocal range. When you visit this tool, you’ll find instructional videos, detailed definitions of voice range registers and voice types, and additional tools to help you identify your vocal range in the best way possible.

Finding Your Vocal Range Can Be Easy

Now that you can identify how wide your vocal range is, you can focus on taking care of your voice and avoid singing uncomfortable notes. When it comes to tools available online, it doesn’t end here. There are plenty more tools you can use to help you musically.
