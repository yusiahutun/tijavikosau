Kiota School fees' policies

  • All fees must be paid in full and in advance before the first day of the term.
  • Bank slips should be emailed or presented at the school with a straightforward narrative.
  • Cash payments are not accepted at the school.
  • A penalty fee will be charged for late payment of fees after opening school.
  • A 5% tuition fee discount is given to a parent with a 2nd child in the school and 7.5% for a 3rd child. The discount is awarded to the child of a lower class.
  • A 5% tuition fee discount is given if school fees are settled in full by the 15th of every holiday month.
  • Music, recorder lessons, computer classes, and physical education are part of the curriculum; no extra fees apply.
  • Swimming and French are included for primary students at no extra charge.

Kiota School performance

Throughout the years, the school has maintained an impressive performance in KCPE. Excellent performance stems from the fact that teachers build on the unique talents of each learner. In addition, the institute has been positively reviewed by many.

Kiota School branches

The school has three campuses: Dennis Pritt, Kasuku, and Karen. Discover below the type of students each campus caters to:

Dennis Pritt campus

The campus caters to kids ages 2 - 6 years, and the classes offered are as follows:

  • Playgroup (2-3 year old pupils)
  • KG1 (3-4 year old pupils)
  • PP1 (4-5 year old pupils)
  • PP2 (5-6 year old pupils)

Kasuku campus

The Kasuku campus caters for learners in:

  • Primary school - Grade 1 to Grade 6
  • Junior Secondary School - Grade 7
  • Cambridge - Grade 7

Karen campus

Kiota School Karen campus caters to kindergarten and primary school learners.

Is Kiota School an international school?

What are the values of the Kiota School?

The school believes in providing an environment where children thrive and nurturing them with Christian and Biblical principles.

What age is a playgroup for in Kenya?

Playgroup in Kenya is for children ages 2 to 3 years. Since some children take longer to adjust to the playgroup life, only take your child to playgroup when he/she is ready.

What is the best age to start preschool?

The appropriate preschool age for a child is three to four. However, consider the child's readiness more than age before moving him/her from playgroup to preschool.
