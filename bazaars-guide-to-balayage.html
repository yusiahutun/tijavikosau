What is balayage and should you have it? Here's Bazaar's guide to balayage

February 2024 · 3 minute read

It's been the most searched for hair colouring technique for two years running and is the favoured choice of Rosie Huntington-Whiteley, Jessica Biel and Chrissy Teigen, but what exactly is balayage? We spoke to the experts to find out.

What is balayage?

A French word meaning 'to sweep' or 'to paint', balayage is a technique that gives you a sun-kissed, natural-looking hair colour with less obvious regrowth lines. The freehand, painterly technique allows your colourist to bespoke your look.

How it works:

As it's a freehand technique, no foils or meche are used to create the highlights. "Balayage pieces should be very close and soft at the root leading to a thicker highlight at the ends of the hair" says colourist and original balayage pioneer Jack Howard. To avoid bold and fear-inducing stripes of colour, "it's applied on the surface of hair rather than saturated through it until the very tips."

Will it suit me?

"Balayage is a carefree technique that in my opinion works for everyone, especially those with freer textured hair as its adds personalisation to the style", reveals celebrity hair colourist Josh Wood. As for your natural hair colour, the subtle, sun-kissed look works just as well on dark hair as it does on lighter hues, especially as many colourists, including Hannah Gaboardi at Salon Sloane, "pay particular attention to skin tone and face shape", for a natural yet glamorous glossy finish that feels bespoke to you. "The only thing to watch is this technique won't cover grey – it will only blend them in", Wood cautions.

Does it take long?

Colour doesn't need as much time to develop with balayage, so you can often lighten up in your lunch hour. It does depend on the finished look you'd like to achieve though, as Wood points out: "I have clients that literally take me two minutes to add a few strands of sun kissed lights and others that [need a more involved] process that can take several hours."

Does it last?

Balayage looks natural when it grows out, so doesn't need as much maintenance as traditional highlights. This means "there is very little upkeep with most types of balayage – especially if you go for something like baby lights (which is very subtle) that will give you very small regrowth", reveals Wood. To keep that salon-fresh look, though, Howard recommends an appointment every eight-12 weeks compared to 6 weeks with other methods.

Can it cause any damage?

"Not in the slightest", confirms Wood. "In fact today when you incorporate an additive conditioner to the lightener, like Redken's PH Bonder, £15.07, the colour is strengthening the hair while its lifting."

Is it expensive?

"There are many different interpretations of balayage so the cost is something that should be discussed with your stylist and a fee agreed beforehand", says Wood. Expect to pay upwards of £100 and often more than traditional highlights.
