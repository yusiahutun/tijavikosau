The 12 Best Eyeshadow Palettes, Tested by Us

May 2024 · 19 minute read

Picking an eyeshadow palette is one of the most fun parts of a beauty routine — but just because it’s fun doesn’t mean it’s easy. It’s important to choose one that you’ll not only use regularly, but will also stay perfect even through sweat, tears, and just everyday life. With so many palettes launching daily, finding one that fits your needs and lifestyle can be a challenge. That’s why InStyle tested 32 different eyeshadow palettes in our lab, narrowing the original selection down to the 12 best based on shade range, pigmentation, application, and value. Anastasia Beverly Hills Soft Glam Eyeshadow Palette came out on top for its long-lasting pigment, buttery feel, and versatility.

Honestly, we won’t be shocked when you realize you need more than one of the best picks below. Keep reading for the 12 best eyeshadow palettes.

Our Testing Process

Going down the rabbit hole of eyeshadow palettes, InStyle editors sifted through hundreds of palettes online and in-store to find the 32 highest-rated products to test. Once selected, our in-house lab testers reviewed each palette, keeping in mind the shade range, pigmentation, application, and value. Each of the aforementioned categories was rated on a scale of 1 to 5. After applying an eyeshadow primer (many testers used the Rare Beauty Always An Optimist Weightless Eyeshadow Primer) each tester swatched shades on their arms and eyelids to examine the texture, transfer ability, and fallout of each palette. From these qualities, the products were given a rating with an overall score to ultimately determine the 12 best eyeshadow palettes.

What to Keep in Mind

Colors

Before purchasing a new eyeshadow palette, take inventory of the products that you already own — this will help you see which colors you use the most, along with what shades you might have missing from your collection. From there you can refresh your eyeshadow stash (it does expire after all) or if you're looking to be more adventurous and add some color, look towards your closet and consider looking for palettes that match your wardrobe. If you want your makeup to be the main character of your look, try bold palettes with a lot of color like Juvia's Place Warrior III Palette or Alamar Cosmetics Spanglish Pressed Pigment Palette. If you like blushed tones then Charlotte Tilbury Instant Eye Palette in Pillow Talk is for you, whereas Too Faced Born This Way The Natural Nudes Eye Shadow Palette has neutral shades for all, no matter your outfit or skin tone.

Finish

Are you glitter-obsessed? Do you prefer neutral mattes? Or maybe you want a little of everything! Take a look at the eyeshadow colors you currently have to determine what finish you use the most. Satin, metallic, shimmer, and glitter are only a few examples of the finish type found in eyeshadow palettes. Most of the options on this list, like PYT Beauty The Upcycle Eyeshadow Palette in Cool Crew Nude, include a mix of matte and shimmer shades for versatile use, specifically for those who like a combination. If you’re hitting the pan on matte shades faster than others, consider Dose of Colors Baked Browns Palette for all your transition shade needs. For those who like glitz and glam, a palette full of sparkly shades, like ColourPop Truly Madly Deeply Pressed Powder Eyeshadow Palette, is for you.

Size

When picking out an eyeshadow palette think like Goldilocks (minus the burglary) to find one that isn’t too big, or too small. There is a just right option for you and it will depend on your space. If you like to keep things minimal and don’t have the room for a big palette, Dose of Colors Baked Browns Palette and Maybelline New York The Nudes Wear Eyeshadow Palette will fit into small makeup bags or drawers. If you like to display your makeup and want to make it a focal part of your desk or makeup bag, Pat McGrath Labs Mothership IX: Huetopian Dream Eyeshadow Palette is a hefty choice with a sleek look that you’ll want everyone to see. Again, this is the time to take stock of your makeup collection to see what space you will have available. Another thing to keep in mind is your traveling habits. Do you keep makeup with you on your commute to work? How much do you usually pack for a trip? Consider these things before picking a palette to make sure it’ll fit in with your lifestyle.

Your Questions, Answered

How long do eyeshadow palettes last?

Most eyeshadow palettes will last anywhere from 12 to 18 months, depending on the product and the brand that makes it. Every skincare, haircare, and makeup product you own has an expiration date — you can discover your product's expiration on the back of the packaging. There will be an icon of an unopened jar followed by a number and the letter M: The letter and number combination stands for how many months the product is good for after opening. To avoid eye irritation and other skin concerns, stop using any eyeshadow product after its expiration date.

How do you apply an eyeshadow palette?

There are no rules to makeup! Okay, maybe there are some rules — especially when it comes to eye-specific products. Make sure to always use eye-safe products and eyeshadows around your eyes, this is especially important with glitter cosmetics that might cause irritation. (AKA keep the craft glitter at home.)

Eyeshadow application can take practice if you want a specific look  — like an intense smokey eye or cut-crease look. To start, consider priming your eyelids to get the best payoff possible and longer-lasting makeup. This will help the pigment stick, as well as create an even foundation on discolored eyelids. For application, you’ll also want to think about the finish of the colors so you can determine if a brush or your fingers are the best tools. For shimmer, metallic, and glitter shades your finger will be your best friend to get your desired results. Once you start blending, a small buffing brush will help sheer out shades for whatever eye look you’re creating.

The order you do your makeup can be beneficial to your shadow application, especially if your palette contains shadows that will transfer onto your under-eye. This prompts one of the most debated questions in the makeup world: Whether eyeshadow goes first or foundation. This vital step comes down to preference, but Loiz knows that many prefer doing eye-makeup first to account for fall out. No matter the look or order, Loiz and I both agree, "In the end, if the whole face looks beautiful, it doesn't matter which order you do the eye makeup!"
