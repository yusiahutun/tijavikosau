Uhuru Kenyatta Omits William Ruto in His Madaraka Day Celebrations Speech

June 2024 · 2 minute read
  • President Uhuru Kenyatta led Kenyans in marking Kenya's 59th Madaraka Day celebrations at Uhuru Gardens
  • During the celebrations, the protocol was ignored after the presidential master of ceremony proceeded to invite Uhuru to make his speech
  • This was a departure from the norm where Deputy President William Ruto invites the President to make his address

There was a clear change of protocol during the Madaraka Day celebrations after Deputy President William Ruto failed to make an address during the national fete.

Madaraka Day fete at Uhuru Gardens

President Uhuru Kenyatta led Kenyans in the marking of this year's Madaraka Day which is also his last national event as president.

During the ceremony, there was a notable dismissal of protocol after Ruto and other leading dignitaries failed to speak at Uhuru Gardens where thousands of Kenyans gathered to mark Kenya's 59th Madaraka Day.

Even more eyebrow-raising was the fact that the deputy president did not also invite the president to make his address as is the norm and protocol in national events.

The head of head was invited to the podium to speak by the presidential master of ceremony.

Before making his speech, Uhuru recognised various leaders and he also blatantly failed to recognise Ruto but went ahead to acknowledge the presence of the two Speakers and Chief Justice Martha Koome.

Uhuru invited his Sierra Leona counterpart Julius Maada who spoke briefly before the head of state resumed his speech.

It is worthy to note that other dignitaries who would ordinarily speak during a national event did not also get the chance.

Uhuru fails to invite Ruto to state house meeting

TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported that Ruto was not invited to the early morning state house meeting between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Cabinet secretaries.

Before leaving for Uhuru Gardens for the Madaraka Day national celebrations, Uhuru met Cabinet members and thanked them for their exemplary work in the dockets.

However, conspicuously missing was the deputy president who is a Cabinet member and according to his aide Emmanuel Tallam, he did not receive an invitation to the Cabinet meeting.

"He wasn't invited to the State House event,” Talam said.
