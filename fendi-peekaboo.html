Where to buy the Fendi Peekaboo

April 2024 · 5 minute read

Most of us are probably guilty of walking around with an unfastened bag; after all, who has the time to secure the clasp every time you retrieve your phone/glasses/book/lipstick from its depths? Silvia Venturini Fendi surely had this mind when she dreamt up the Peekaboo, a bag that looks even better when left open.

As the artistic director presiding over Fendi’s hallowed accessories line, Venturini Fendi knows a thing or two about creating bags that excite. Her first hit? The inimitable Fendi Baguette, of course. Launched in 1997 and made famous by Sex and the City, this little bag was at the centre of Noughties' logomania (and continues to incite desire as the Y2K revival rages on).

Created as the antithesis to shouty 'It' bags, the Peekaboo was decidedly more discreet and practically logo-less, with a comparatively minimal design. It defined ‘quiet luxury’ well before the term trended.

"There was an invasion of bags on the catwalk everywhere at the time, so I really wanted to create a bag that only a real leather goods maker like Fendi could possibly make," says Venturini Fendi. "It was the moment to set things straight with a new classic."

The Peekaboo first appeared in raw cotton toile, glossy patent leather and perforated suede at Fendi’s spring/summer 2009 show, as the accessory to Karl Lagerfeld’s ready-to-wear collection. The name referred to the traditional children's game, 'Peekaboo, I see you'. Initially, the bag was called the 'Hide and Seek' but Venturini Fendi felt that Peekaboo was a better fit for the way the interior compartments of the bag are revealed.

The bag's top handle and trapezium-shaped form pay homage to mid-century styles but with a modern twist – quite literally. Thanks to its clever construction and weighted keyhole hardware, a simple swivel of the turnlock clasp creates a drape (or ‘smile’) that reveals a beautifully crafted interior – a contrasting colour, precious material, or, in the case of 2013’s ‘Monster’ Peekaboo, a pair of piercing yellow eyes.

"Carrying a Peekaboo says something about a very strong, powerful and empowered person who is not afraid to hide," says Venturini Fendi. "It has its two compartments – one you can keep close to you and closed, and the other you can choose to reveal something of yourself to the world."

Estrop//Getty Images

From royals to recording artists, the bag has won fans in a whole host of influential women. Queen Rania of Jordan, Amal Clooney and Meghan Markle wear theirs with neutral tailoring, while Rihanna and Hailey Bieber tote theirs with crisp, indigo denim. Artist Tracey Emin and the late architect Zaha Hadid were among those who put their own spin on the Peekaboo for a 2014 charity auction; Hadid’s was all-black and enwrapped with layers, like the pages of a book.

Since its launch, the Peekaboo has been rethought in almost every material you can imagine. It has been sprayed with sequins, made into a yellow-eyed, red-mouthed ‘Monster’, and even encrusted with diamonds, rubies and amethysts. There are reoccurring, signature updates, too, such as the signature Selleria hand-stitching and woven, ‘interlaced’ leather – this season, reimagined in luxurious, hand-applied shearling.

In 2018, to celebrate the bag’s 10th anniversary, Fendi released the more lightweight, single-pocket Peekaboo X-Lite, while 2020 saw the introduction of the Peekaboo ISeeU – a graphic, accordion-like design with an interchangeable pouch. All three models, including the original rendition, have been scaled up and down. Those wanting to accessorise their Peekaboo need look no further than the Nano, a miniature version that can be added as a charm or worn as a bag in its own (tiny) right.

The openness of the Peekaboo speaks to Fendi’s confidence in craftsmanship. As anyone can see, the bag is as beautiful on the inside as it is on the outside – the result of hours of handwork. First, the finest leather is selected and hand-cut with meticulous care and precision. In the case of the Peekaboo Interlace, up to 900 holes are hand-drilled, via which fine strips of leather are woven – also by hand. There is the signature Selleria stitching – only ever executed manually – and the painstakingly hand-painted edges. As for the all-important ‘peekaboo’ lining, it is chosen for its softness and beauty. "When you put your hands inside a Peekaboo, you can feel some of the softest leather in the world," says Venturini Fendi.

Jeremy Moeller//Getty Images

In the throes of the quiet luxury revolution, the Peekaboo seems more relevant than ever. If it’s taught us anything, it’s that leaving one bag’s open isn’t lazy, it’s chic – and that what’s on the inside most certainly counts.

The Peekaboo turns 15 this year, and – in celebration – Fendi is releasing 'The Peekaboo-k', a coffee table tome that looks at the fascinating history of the bag, featuring a photo collection of 80 bags from the historical archive. So, if you want to discover even more about one of the house's most iconic bags, head here.
