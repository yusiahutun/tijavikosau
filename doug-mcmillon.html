The Origin Story

Doug McMillon was born in Memphis, Tennessee, on the 17th of October 1966.

He is the eldest of three children born to Laura and Morris McMillon.

His father, who had served in Vietnam, now worked as a dentist.

”I think they earned a middle-class income, but they’d grown up without anything,” McMillon told Harvard Magazine about his parents.

C. Doug grew up in Jonesboro in Arkansas, but when he was 16 y.o, his family moved to Bentonville, Arkansas, the home of Walmart headquarters.

This timeline dissects his path to becoming the CEO.

1984 – When C. Doug was just a teenager in high school, he began his first job at Walmart as a summer associate.

Throughout the summer, he was tasked with unloading trucks in a distribution center.

1989 – After high school, C. Doug attended the University of Arkansas, where he graduated with a bachelor’s degree in Business Administration.

1990 – As a postgraduate opportunity, C. Doug studied for a Masters of Business Administration (MBA) at the University of Tulsa.

During his studies, he reached out to an executive working at Walmart and told them he was interested in becoming a buyer once he had completed his studies.

He was swiftly offered an assistant manager role at a Walmart store in Tulsa, in Oklahoma.

1991 – After completing his MBA, C. Doug moved up the ladder by moving back to Bentonville and joining the buyer-training program at the Walmart Headquarters.

Initially, he was tasked with buying fishing tackle but took on various roles as a merchandiser and a buyer, dealing in clothes, crafts, food, and furnishings.

With time, he later moved on to work as a general merchandise manager for Sam’s Club, Walmart’s wholesale store division.

2005 – C. Doug was promoted to President and CEO of Sam’s Club on the 4th of August 2005.
