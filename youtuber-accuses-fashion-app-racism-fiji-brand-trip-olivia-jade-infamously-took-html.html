YouTuber accuses fashion app of racism on Fiji brand trip that Olivia Jade infamously took

September 2024 · 6 minute read

A black YouTuber has accused a fashion shopping app of racism after she went on a brand trip to Fiji — which is the same trip Olivia Jade infamously attended during her first week of college. 

Kianna Naomi, 19, shared her experience working for Dote Shopping in a YouTube video on Saturday — where she detailed why she felt like an 'outcast' during the entire brand trip. 

'I didn’t talk about the way I was treated for a number of reasons,' Kianna said at the start of her video. 'I didn’t want to be the problematic black girl, and I didn’t want to be labeled as ungrateful because I’m literally in the most beautiful country there is.' 

Upset: YouTuber Kianna Naomi, 19, shared her experience with Dote Shopping on a brand trip

Tropical vacation: The app invited a group of women on a brand trip to Fiji in August. While on the brand trip, Kianna said she felt like an 'outcast' based on her race

Enjoying the sunshine: She claimed that on the brand trip, where the focus is taking pictures, the photographers ignored her 

In August, Dote invited a group of women down to Fiji to promote the app while also enjoying the country. Kianna, who has 740,000 subscribers on YouTube, was one of the women on the trip — including stars like Olivia Jade and Emma Chamberlain. 

While Kianna felt 'grateful' to be invited on the brand trip, she also felt like the 'token black girl' for the company to promote diversity in future brand pictures. 

'I’m so glad I got to experience it, but I wish it hadn’t been with this company,' she said. 

The YouTuber went on to say she was not upset specifically about anyone who was on the trip. Instead, she was frustrated with how she felt she was treated by the brand regarding her race. 

'I didn’t speak up because I was protecting a company that didn’t care about my well-being in the slightest,' she said.  

'I thought that I could be representation for black girls all over the world. But for me to feel like the token black girl on two trips in a row; it’s dead. I don’t want to do it anymore.' 

One of the biggest issues mentioned by Kianna in her 16-minute video was the lack of incentive the brand had to feature her in pictures or get her photographed while in Fiji. Since the trip was largely focused on taking pictures, Kianna said she felt like an 'outcast' because the photographers had allegedly no interest in shooting with her.  

'No one is taking pictures of you on a trip that is about pictures, you’re going to feel very uncomfortable,' she said. 

When speaking to DailyMail.com, a Dote spokesperson later clarified that only two photo shoots were scheduled during the trip. Outside those photo shoots, it was up to the photographers and attendees to schedule individual sessions.

Kianna said she addressed concerns about photo shoots with the event organizers, who then organized a separate photo shoot on the beach with a group of women. But Kianna said they pictures were never used by the brand and instead just 'discarded' by the photographer.

'I know what you’re thinking, honestly. “It’s not that big of a deal, it doesn’t matter,"' she continued in the video. 'But I want to let you guys know it’s not about getting pictures in Fiji. It’s about feeling unwelcome, it’s about feeling uncomfortable, it’s about feeling like an outcast.' 

Promoting diversity in the social media industry is a priority Kianna hopes many brands will focus on in the coming years. But she felt there lacked an understanding of how to address diversity needs from Dote. 

'Diversity is not throwing one black girl among a whole bunch of white ones and being like, "Aye we’re diverse,"' she said. 

'I don’t think this company knows how to handle women of color, and I don’t think they want to.' 

Statements by Kianna against the company come as multiple women have called out the brand for lacking diversity. These claims were ignited after Coachella when the brand was accused of separating nonwhite girls with white girls in the home they rented for the California festival. 

DailyMail.com contacted Dote about the controversy with the Fiji trip and the claims it was exclusive to specific attendees. 

Revealed: In a statement, Dote said: 'We have always gone to great lengths to ensure that everyone is treated with dignity and respect.' The brand also said it made changes to its trips

In a statement shared by a Dote spokesperson, the company said: 'Dote’s mission is to foster diversity, inclusivity and equality, and we would never do anything deliberately to hinder that objective. Dote has hosted several trips for our brand ambassadors, and we have always gone to great lengths to ensure that everyone is treated with dignity and respect.'

The statement continued with the brand saying it was 'saddened that some creators and commentators have misconstrued some of the facts.'

'Are we perfect? Of course not. We have learned from every trip and have done our best to make improvements to every experience,' the company continued. 'We take constructive comments very seriously, and we continually strive to build a community that promotes and celebrates diversity and equality.'

Kianna said she met with someone from the Dote team when leaving Fiji to discuss ways the company could change how it handled brand trips to better accommodate all attendees. 

A Dote spokesperson said the company took her suggestions seriously and later altered how photo shoots were scheduled to make sure no influencer felt they lacked time with a photographer. The company also employed a change to its photographer policies. 

Lori Loughlin's daughter Olivia Jade, 19, was another influencer on the brand trip. The YouTuber shared a number of snaps of herself on the beach during the tropical vacation, which was also during her first week of classes at the University of Southern California. 

At the time, no one knew Olivia and her family would later become embroiled in controversy regarding how the young YouTuber got into college. In March, it was revealed that Lori and her husband, Mossimo Giannulli, paid $500,000 to get both of their children into USC. 

The two daughters have since left the Los Angeles university while their parents face criminal charges.
