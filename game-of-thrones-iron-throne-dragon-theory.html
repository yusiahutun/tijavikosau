Game of Thrones Teases Who Ends Up on Iron Throne in Season 8 Poster

June 2024 · 3 minute read

Warning: This post may contain spoilers for Game of Thrones.

  • HBO released a new poster for Game of Thrones Season 8 this week, showing the Iron Throne fused into the face of a dragon.
  • Some fans are speculating whether the imagery hints that a Targaryen (either Daenerys or Jon) will end up as ruler of the Seven Kingdoms.
  • Others are wondering if it means a dragon will destroy the throne altogether.

HBO has been tightly secretive with the final season of Game of Thrones, but fans have been making do with the little clues they've been given. After the network released a new poster for the show's upcoming season this week, GoT enthusiasts focused on some enticing details. The graphic shows a closeup of the swords making up the Iron Throne fusing into the face of a dragon. Considering its black scales and red eyes, it's likely Drogon, one of Daenerys Targaryen's dragons, which was named after her former husband, Drogo.

Although the poster didn't say much—besides "GOT" and the Season 8 release date (April 14)—some fans still interpreted the illustration in an interesting way: If there's a dragon connected to the throne in the poster, does that mean a Targaryen will end up on the throne by the end of the show?

HBO

While it's easy to think this points to Daenerys's future as Queen of the Seven Kingdoms, remember Jon Snow is technically a Targaryen as well. In the Season 7 finale, we found out that his true parents were Lyanna Stark (Ned's sister) and Rhaegar Targaryen (Daenerys's brother), who were secretly married, making him the heir to the throne.

Fans speculated on Twitter.

One user pointed out the possibility of Jon and Daenerys ruling together, since the poster shows two sword pommels in the center.

Another (less optimistic) outcome could be that the throne could be destroyed altogether. As a refresher, the Iron Throne was created by the early Targaryen king Aegon the Conqueror, who collected the swords of his opponents and melded them together with the fiery breath of his dragon, Balerion the Black Dread. Is it possible only a dragon can destroy the structure?

Ultimately, it's possible the poster is completely unrelated to the plot, just like how the "Crypts of Winterfell" teaser released in January had "nothing to do with the series," according to Sophie Turner (Sansa Stark). Looks like we won't find out until GoT is back on air in a month.
