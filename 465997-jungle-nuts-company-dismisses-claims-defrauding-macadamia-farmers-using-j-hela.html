Jungle Nuts Company Dismisses Claims of Defrauding Macadamia Farmers using J-HELA

May 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Jungle Nuts Limited buys macadamia directly from farmers mainly located in the Mount Kenya region
  • All was well until 2015 when the company introduced a new system dubbed J-HELA to pay their farmers
  • After some time, the farmers' J-HELA accounts which had millions of money, started being closed, raising eyebrows

Jungle Nuts Limited, a company linked to Kiambu gubernatorial seat candidate Patrick Wainaina is on the spot for allegedly defrauding farmers' millions of shillings.

The Thika-based establishment buys macadamia directly from small-scale farmers mainly located in Kiambu, Murang’a and other parts of the country.

A spot check by TUKO.co.ke established that the company buys a kilogram of the produce between KSh 60 and KSh 80 and makes payments at the end of the month.

The macadamia is then processed and exported to the United States, China and the United Kingdom.

J-HELA

Initially, farmers welcomed the initiative saying it would give them good returns.

However, in 2015, things started going south when the company introduced a new payment system dubbed J-HELA.

The system was to replace the M-Pesa and bank payments that the company originally used to compensate the macadamia farmers.

The new system was heavily criticised by farmers, some saying it was not user-friendly.

"We once called a meeting to demand the scraping off of J-HELA but we were told it was the only suitable payment method as it could enable them to transfer even over KSh 70,000, which was not possible through M-Pesa then," said one farmer.

Having no money withdrawing option, the farmers, after receiving their returns on the platform, were then required to transfer them to their M-Pesa or bank, which was very costly.

Accounts closed

In a dramatic turn of events, the farmers' J-HELA accounts which had millions of money, started being closed.

At the moment, the farmers claim, J-HELA system is withholding millions of money from its farmers and users.

The affected farmers lamented that the company stopped its operations in Meru and withheld their dues.

"We have lost a lot of money in this company; we had a bonus but were not paid. We no longer see them here. I have lost hope," said one elderly Meru farmer.

Company's stand

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, the company dismissed the claims saying the company is very operational and no money has been lost.

The company said they opted to use J-HELA because the system is very efficient, adding that paying farmers through M-Pesa was more time-consuming.

"There was a necessity of having J-HELA where we are very sure that anyone using that application is our worker and is involved with us and if there is any money that needs to be credited to their account, we know about it. On the issue charges, any payment system usually has its costs," said the company.
