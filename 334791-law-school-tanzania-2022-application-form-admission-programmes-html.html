The Law School of Tanzania was established in 2007 under the Ministry of Constitutional Affairs and Justice. It replaced an Externship system with a Practical Legal Training Program. Would you love to apply for admissions at the Law School of Tanzania in 2022?

The Law School is of Tanzania offers practical legal programs to the undergraduate law degree graduates. The school has two main departments, the office of the principal and the research and publication department.

Law School of Tanzania's programs, application and admission

Start researching about the Law School of Tanzania's programs, admissions, and applications around a year or two before enrolling. Also, submit your application before the deadlines to give yourself ample time to complete all the necessary steps.

Academic programs

The Law School of Tanzania offers a one-year training program, which is compulsory for everyone who wants to became an advocate of the High Court of Tanzania. The course has two semesters:

  • Semester two covers about 36 weeks of field placement or clinical law, followed by written and oral exams. You will receive a Post-Graduate Diploma in Legal Practice after passing the exams.

Application

The Law School of Tanzania 2021/2022 intake's admission was on 10th to 14th January 2022 and their first semester's academic timetable is already out. Therefore, they are not accepting applications for admission for now.

Also, note that your application is only verified after paying a TZS 20,000 non-refundable application fee. You can always find out when applications will be open by doing this:

  • Visit the Law School of Tanzania's website.
  • Tap click here below the ONLINE APPLICATION section's image.
  • The Student information system will open.
  • An application form will appear if the process is ongoing.
  • Click on How to Apply to access a pdf demonstrating the steps with images. The document also contains requirements for application, how to make payments, and payment options.

Payments

Since the institution does not accept cash, deposit money to its designated bank account or use four mobile payment options, Tigopesa, M-Pesa, AirtelMoney, and T-Pesa. The payments' portal on the website is reserved for Law School of Tanzania's staff to fills in your name, email, and phone number, then gives you a control number and a billing invoice printout in pdf.

Requirements for application

Ideally, send your application while in your last year of college if you want to enter law school immediately after graduating. Meanwhile, get these documents in preparation for application:

1. All applicants from postgraduate programs (form IV and form VI holders) should have both A- level and O- level index numbers written in this format, S0000/0000/0000 or EQ2014002504/2001 (eg. S1147/0152/2012).

  • Add a zero (0) to a certificate that has three digits to make 4 digits. eg. S152/228/2015 to S0152/0228/2015.

2. Equivalent applicants and Foundation programs (Diploma holders) should have an Award verification number (AVN) from NACTE.

  • You can obtain the number online from NACTE's website at a fee. An Example of AVN is(19NA12212ME). Do not upload any certificate.

3. Obtain NACTE's translation if you have foreign certificates ie Diploma for O level and A Level certificates and TCU for degrees.

4. Apply online using names that appear in your form four (O level) certificates. Don't alter their sequence or add extra names.

5. Provide the correct information when applying. Any non-disclosure of details or providing false information will cancel your admission.

    Admission and requirements

    Those do not register for admission by due date forfeit their offer of admission. Therefore, be among the first to find out about the school's admission datelines, timetables and other announcements by following these steps:

  • Visit the school's website.
  • Click on Information.
  • Select News & Events on the dropdown list.
  • Download and open files related to applications, admissions, and timetables for updated information.
    • Selected candidates are notified through their application accounts. Therefore, the office of the registrar publishes the user account password in one of the documents attached on the website.
    • Always check your status and admission letter from your account and download the admission letter.
    • For registration purposes, bring your admission letter, original copies of all academic certificates, transcripts (duly issued, signed, and sealed), and birth certificate/National ID used in the application process.
    • Other details are provided in the admission letter.
