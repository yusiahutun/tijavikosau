What it's like to holiday like Taylor Swift on Australia's Hamilton Island

June 2024 Â· 10 minute read

You don't have to score a number one album to holiday like a superstar.

As many well-heeled Aussies know, the uber-luxurious privately owned Hamilton Island is possibly the most picturesque place to get away from it all - and is conveniently located just off of the country's Queensland coast.

Hamilton, which is situated along the Great Barrier Reef, is also the largest of the inhabited Whitsunday Islands, known for their pristine white-sand beaches and crystal clear water. So it should probably come as no surprise that the resort isle counts Taylor Swift among its fans.

Hamilton Island is a five kilometre-square island located in the Whitsundays, in Queensland, and is home to four resorts, including the famed Qualia

A quick golf buggy drive from Qualia, guests can find Hamilton Island's quaint village and marina, which is home to a bakery and chip shop

Taylor Swift managed to fit in some rest and relaxation during the Australian leg of her 1989 World Tour on the famed Hamilton Island. Pictured: the singer making friends with one of the many cockatoos that call the island home

Qualia's Beach House has a 10-person dining area, kitchenette, spacious bedroom and infinity-edge plunge pool overlooking the Coral Sea

Just last year, Taylor, along with her family and 125 members of her tour crew, spent several days staying on Hamilton - specifically, at its famed Qualia hotel and resort - and exploring the nearby reef.

But lounging in the perpetual sunshine isn't all there is to do on the island - it's a water sport lovers paradise.

Between the scuba diving, snorkelling, sailing and helicopter tours, visitors can quite literally experience the Great Barrier Reef from all angles.

And, of course, no visit to Australia is complete without the requisite snap with a koala or kangaroo. Thankfully, Hamilton Island makes it easy thanks to its Wild Life Sanctuary. Handlers will bring the adorable marsupials to your hotel grounds for a photo op, or alternatively, you can book in to have breakfast with them instead.

On the island, visitors should also check out the WILD LIFE sanctuary for a chance to cuddle a koala. Pictured: MailOnline Travel's Katie

'Thank you to the Australian Wildlife Conservation Fund for letting us hang out with Willy. WE LOVE HIM and will miss him terribly,' Taylor wrote alongside this family holiday snap

Qualia's most expensive pavilion, the Beach House, which runs from Â£2400 per night, is one of the most luxurious villas on the island

Although it's unknown which of Qualia's 60 pavilions Taylor stayed in, it's believed that it may have been the Windward Pavilion (pictured)

In the Windward Pavilion, a sprawling bathroom features a statement-making tub, as well as a separate shower area

Another luxury room option available at Qualia is the lavish Leeward Pavilion, which overlooks the stunning Whitsunday waters

Both the Windward Pavilion (pictured) and the Leeward Pavilion have open-air lounges, expansive sundecks and private plunge pools

We arrived on Hamilton Island after an overnight Reefsleep on a pontoon in the middle of the sea, so we were eager to hit the island's many gorgeous beaches as quickly as possible.

However, our first order of business was to visit Qualia, the island's most popular - and most exclusive - resort, situated on Hamilton's northernmost point. (Also the hotel that Taylor Swift and her entourage stayed in, of course.)

Although it's unknown which of Qualia's luxurious villas Taylor and her crew opted for, it's believed that the singer stayed in either the Beach House, Windward Pavilion or Leeward Pavilion. None would be a bad choice.

However, one of the resort's most secluded choices, Beach House, is truly unique - and also the most expensive at $5,000 AUD (Â£2,400) and $6,000 AUD (Â£2,900) per night during the peak holiday season.

Not only does it boast an elegant open-air 10-person dining area, but there is also a private infinity-edge swimming pool from which to take in the stunning views.

Designed in a light and airy open-plan style, the central living room likewise opens to the panoramic vista of the Coral Sea.

The main house features one spacious bedroom, as well as a stone bathroom with separate shower, but the one-of-a-kind room also has an adjoining guest pavilion. The guesthouse can sleep two - in either a king or twin beds - and has its own en suite and private deck, as well 

The resort's Pebble Beach is home to both a restaurant serving fresh seafood and also the launching point for many water sport activities

There is also the Qualia spa, which is known for its 'Bularri Yarrul' hot stone massage, which uses 300 million year old stones

Upon check in, all Qualia guests will be greeted with this breathtaking view of the Whitsunday Islands from the Long Pavilion

Also in Long Pavilion is a bar, lounge area, plunge pool and fine dining restaurant, known for its ever-evolving tasting menu

For those who prefer to venture outside of the privacy of their pavilion, Pebble Beach also offers an infinity pool for all guests to enjoy

And if you'd rather experience private, sunset dining, Pebble Beach's accomplished chefs can set that up for you, as well

Namaste! And finally, the resort's yoga pavilion is the ultimate picturesque spot to relax, unwind and reflect

The word Qualia itself means 'a collection of deeper sensory experiences' - and the hotel sure does live up to its name.

Crafted from timber, sandstone and glass, the distinctively Australian resort is home to 60 private pavilions, yet you'd never know, as they are all discreetly hidden away behind lush greenery and strategic gates.

And while it's entirely possible to simply keep to yourself in such a secluded villa - many of which come complete with plunge pools and sundecks - there's also plenty more to experience throughout the resort and island.

Fine dining options are available at Long Pavilion or Pebble Beach, which is where we tucked into a feast of fresh seafood that included chilled king prawns, local reef fish, seared scallops, oysters and grilled Whitsunday bugs. (For those who don't know, bugs are slipper lobsters, closely related to Rocklobsters.)

Pebble Beach also boasts a large infinity pool and cocktail bar where unique concoctions, such as banana coladas, can be whipped up on request.

And those sandy shores are Qualia's base for all water sports, so if you were ever going to opt for a sea-kayaking adventure on the Coral Sea, this is probably the place you'll want to do it.

As well as Pebble Beach, the Hamilton Island marina is also a launching-off point for day-long catamaran and sailing trips

While on Hamilton, Katie stayed at the Reef View Hotel, where rooms offered unparalleled views of Catseye Beach and the Coral Sea

At Reef View, many balconies overlook the stunning Whitsunday Islands - and cockatoos often make themselves at home along the rail

Whitehaven may be the most famous, but Catseye Beach, one of the main beaches on the island, is beautiful in its own right 

While visiting Hamilton Island, we took a chartered yacht to a secluded, private island several hours from the bustling resort

The views from the many hilltops on the island are not to be missed - especially One Tree Hill, where you can enjoy a drink as well

Outside of Qualia's confines, there are also plenty of other accommodation options.

There's the adult-only Beach Club, located right on Catseye Beach, which is comprised of 57 luxurious rooms and its own child-free restaurant and lounge.

We, however, stayed at the Reef View Hotel, one of the island's tallest buildings, which offers unparalleled Coral Sea Views.

The updated room was comfortable - and the cockatoos who visited the balcony daily were friendly - but, admittedly, we didn't spend much time indoors.

On our second day on Hamilton, we took off in a golf buggy - the quickest way to get around the five square-kilometre island - to explore. The relatively unmarked roads, however, do offer a bit of a navigation challenge. Still, you probably don't want to walk up to the island's famed lookout point, One Tree Hill, on foot. Nevertheless, there's no better place to enjoy a glass of prosecco at sunset.

There's also a bustling marina, as well as a main street with shops, restaurants and even a nightclub. Here you can buy everything from sun cream to iced coffee, but beware: items that have been brought on to Hamilton do cost more than they would on the mainland. (So stock up on aloe vera before you arrive.)

Another accommodation option is the adults-only Beach Club, which as its name suggests, is ideally located just steps from the beach

At Beach Club, there is also a private child-free infinity pool, as well as specialised - and very secluded - dining options 

Hamilton Island Golf Club is one of the world's most spectacular courses - after all, where else can you get a view like that from the tee?

Our afternoon was spent on a chartered Sunsail yacht to a private beach - not the famed Whitehaven, unfortunately, due to lack of time. 

It didn't really matter though as the inlet, which we ultimately reached by dinghy, was completely secluded. The uninhabited island was actually home to a National Park, and although visitors could camp, there weren't any when we arrived.

Snorkelling is said to be exceptional in the area, but after 24 hours spent on a pontoon in the sea, we were really ready for some time on the beach. Needless to say, the afternoon passed far too quickly.

That evening, we decided to take advantage of the nightlife that Hamilton has to offer. After a delicious dinner at Asian tapas restaurant, Coca Chu, we visited Marina Tavern for a late-night drink.

The Reef View Hotel also has an impressive lobby bar and the espresso martinis are definitely worth a try.

But it was the following morning that one of the most memorable moments of the trip occurred. Much like Taylor Swift and her family, we were invited to meet - and have a quick cuddle - with an adorable koala at the Beach Club resort.

It was the perfect cap to an idyllic 48 hours in paradise - and also provided me with one of my most-liked Instagram posts of the year.

Taylor and her crew spent an afternoon on Whitehaven Beach, which is known for its stunning white sand and pristine turquoise water

At sunset, guests often head up to One Tree Hill to take advantage of the orange and red sky overlooking the azure Coral Sea

It's also recommended to take a walk up to the highest point on Hamilton - Passage Peak - which offers some of the best Whitsunday views

For those seeking extra adventure, Hamilton Island guests can book a trip out to Hardy Reef's Reefworld pontoon for an afternoon of snorkelling and scuba diving
