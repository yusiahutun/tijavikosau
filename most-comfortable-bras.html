Our Testing Process

After doing some extensive research on the best bras for women, we gathered the 20 most highly rated comfortable bras on the market, and sent them to our esteemed team of editors. Each bra was tested by at least two people with varying body types in order to gauge how the bra performed on small and big chests. Over the course of three weeks, we gathered insights on how the bra felt, lifted, supported, and lasted. We took note of any pokings and discomfort. We then picked the 11 most comfortable bras that outperformed the rest, gathering our experiences here.

What to Keep in Mind

Material

In this story, you will notice that every single one of the mentioned bras are made up of elastane (also referred to as spandex) and either nylon or polyamide. That’s because the most comfortable bras need to have a stretchy element to them that help them mold around the body. When combined with nylon, like in the Aerie SMOOTHEZ Unlined Bra and the ThirdLove 24/7 Second Skin Unlined Bra, the durable fabric allows you to wear the bra on a daily basis without it falling apart. Nylon, which was created as an alternative to silk, also adds a touch of smoothness to the bra that’s appreciated under form-fitting shirts. Polyamide, found in the CUUP The Scoop Bra, also comes with a softness and flexibility that when worn, can feel extraordinarily comfortable. Body-positive model Sophie Hughes says that the comfiest bras she owns are a mix of polyamide and polyester, because they also happen to wash well and retain their quality for some years.

Wiring 

Unless you go completely wireless with a bra like the True & Co. Soft Form V Neck Adjustable Strap Bra option, you’ll want to pay close attention to how the underwire fits you. As a common feature in bras, underwires provide additional support, particularly on bottom-heavy breasts, and lift the breast up. However, when sized incorrectly, they can dig into the skin and cause discomfort. To combat this, some brands have come up with creative engineering to provide underwires that don’t hurt. Take the Aerie SMOOTHEZ Unlined Bra, for example. The underwire is almost undetectable inside the buttery soft material of the band, and we never once had to fidget with it or feel it digging into our chest. For more traditional wiring, like in the SKIMS Fits Everybody T-shirt Bra, the smooth polyamide and elastane band fabric camouflages the wiring adequately to make it feel near-invisible. We also recommend double checking your measurements against size guides to ensure you buy the correct size for you because this is also a surefire way to ensure the bra fits you properly.

Your Questions, Answered

What is the best bra to wear every day?

For a comfortable bra you can wear every day, opt for something that “gives you the right level of support throughout the day,” says Cami Raymond, design director of shapewear brand Ruby Ribbon. Wireless, or barely-there wired bras like the True & Co. Soft Form V Neck Adjustable Strap Bra, Aerie SMOOTHEZ Unlined Bra, and ThirdLove 24/7 Second Skin Unlined Bra are excellent choices for equal parts support and comfort. These more or less seamless styles “give you a smoothing appearance and feel like you’re not wearing a bra at all,” says Raymond, making them more appealing for daily wear. 

How do I choose the most comfortable bra for me?

Different breast shapes, combined with different body types, can make shopping for the most comfortable bra specific to your needs difficult. Bra fitting expert Sabrina Oxford explains that, more than material and style, the most comfortable bra will be the one that fits you properly. “It should feel like a nice hug on your body instead of a hammock for your breasts.” For bottom-heavy breasts, Oxford recommends bras with “more support, allowing you to relieve pressure on your shoulders since you aren’t relying on your straps to hold you up”. We found that options like the CUUP The Scoop Bra and ThirdLove 24/7 Second Skin Unlined Bra, where there was proper lining at the bottom of the bra, did just that for us. 

On petite chests, the discomfort came from tight bands where the smallest cup sizes were still too big. The Pepper Classic All You Bra removed this worry altogether. Raymond chimes in that you want to look for “bras that have adjustable and convertible straps, so you can better customize the fit and avoid gaping.” All bras, with the exception of SPANX Bra-llelujah! Lightly Lined Full Coverage Bra, come with adjustable straps in this story. On top of this, she wants you to consider the size of the straps, as wider straps provide more support and comfort than traditional spaghetti straps. Particularly on heavy chests, this additional support on your shoulders could keep any fabric from digging into your skin. The Wacoal Basic Beauty Spacer Underwire T-Shirt Bra has one of the most supportive wide straps, of the ones we tried on.
