Broadbent’s full name is James Broadbent.

Jim Broadbent, known for his versatility and exceptional acting skills, was born James Broadbent on May 24, 1949, in Lincolnshire, England.

He attended the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art (LAMDA).

Broadbent honed his acting craft at LAMDA, a prestigious drama school in London, where he studied alongside renowned actors such as Anthony Hopkins and Brian Cox.

Broadbent’s breakthrough role came in the 1999 film “Topsy-Turvy.”

In the critically acclaimed “Topsy-Turvy,” directed by Mike Leigh, Broadbent astounded audiences with his portrayal of W.S. Gilbert, earning him widespread recognition and a nomination for Best Actor at the BAFTA Awards.

Broadbent won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.

His outstanding performance as John Bayley, husband of British novelist Iris Murdoch, in the film “Iris” (2001) earned him an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.

He played iconic characters in popular film franchises.

Broadbent showcased his versatility by appearing as Professor Horace Slughorn in the “Harry Potter” series and as Archmaester Ebrose in the hit HBO show “Game of Thrones.”

Broadbent has worked extensively in theater.

His love for the stage led him to perform in numerous theater productions, including the National Theatre’s production of “The Cherry Orchard” and the Royal Shakespeare Company’s “King Lear.”

He received critical acclaim for his role in “Moulin Rouge!”

Broadbent showcased his comedic talents in the musical extravaganza “Moulin Rouge!” (2001), where he played the flamboyant and eccentric owner of the Moulin Rouge nightclub.

Broadbent’s portrayal of Harold Zidler in “Moulin Rouge!” earned him a BAFTA nomination.

His vibrant and captivating performance in “Moulin Rouge!” garnered him a nomination for Best Supporting Actor at the BAFTA Awards.

He appeared alongside Tom Hanks in “Cloud Atlas.”

Broadbent showcased his versatility once again by playing multiple characters in the epic science fiction film “Cloud Atlas” (2012), directed by the Wachowskis and Tom Tykwer.

Broadbent played a key role in the “Bridget Jones” film series.

He portrayed Bridget’s bumbling father, Jim, in the popular romantic comedy franchise, adding comedic charm to the beloved character.

Broadbent’s voice is recognizable in various animated films.

His distinct voice can be heard in animated films like “Shaun the Sheep Movie” (2015) and “The Lady in the Van” (2015).

He has collaborated multiple times with director Mike Leigh.

Jim Broadbent and Mike Leigh formed a successful creative partnership, collaborating on films such as “Life Is Sweet” (1990), “Secrets & Lies” (1996), and “Vera Drake” (2004).

Broadbent appeared in the science fiction TV series “Black Mirror.”

Broadbent won a BAFTA Television Award for his performance in “Longford.”

His portrayal of Lord Longford in the drama film “Longford” (2006) earned him a BAFTA Television Award for Best Actor.

He is known for his collaboration with director Richard Eyre.

Broadbent worked closely with Richard Eyre on projects such as “Iris” (2001) and “Notes on a Scandal” (2006), showcasing their ability to create compelling cinematic experiences.

Broadbent is an accomplished stage actor.

He has received critical acclaim for his stage performances, including his portrayal of Sam Mendes’ “The Winter’s Tale” (2015) and his role as Scrooge in “A Christmas Carol” (2015).

Broadbent appeared in the widely successful film “Hot Fuzz.”

His memorable role as Inspector Frank Butterman in Edgar Wright’s action-comedy “Hot Fuzz” (2007) delighted audiences and showcased his comedic timing.

He played a prominent role in the BBC sitcom “The Street.”

Broadbent captivated viewers with his portrayal of a retired taxi driver in the critically acclaimed British drama series “The Street” (2006-2009).

Broadbent’s performance in “Iris” led to a Golden Globe nomination.

His mesmerizing portrayal of John Bayley in “Iris” earned him a nomination for Best Actor in a Motion Picture – Drama at the Golden Globe Awards.

He received an OBE for his contributions to drama.

In 2002, Broadbent was honored with the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for his outstanding contributions to the field of acting.
