A video of Garissa Township MP Aden Duale calling out Prime Cabinet Secretary nominee Musalia Mudavadi has emerged on social media.

The undated clip resurfaced after the two Kenya Kwanza affiliated leaders underwent vetting before the parliamentary committee.

Duale has been nominated for the Defence Cabinet Secretary position under President William Ruto's government.

"Akiwa Waziri wa local government Musalia alipatikana katika ile kashfa ya makaburi. Alikuwa ameiba makaburi huko machakos. Mtu alienda kuiba makaburi akapanga njama ati watu wafufuliwe ajenge manyumba hapo. Huyo mtu anaweza tuambia maneno ya ufisadi?" Duale posed.

Political doublespeak

In a similar report, Mudavadi used to be a fierce critic of Ruto regarding the latter's role in the Jubilee government.

Mudavadi made a surprise u-turn in January this year during the ANC National Delegates' Convention when he declared he would team up with Ruto's UDA.

However, a video of Mudavadi claiming Ruto was the source of problems affecting Kenyans emerged online a month after the two joined forces.

The former vice president noted the Jubilee politician had been a critical shareholder of President Uhuru Kenyatta's administration thus, it would be hypocritical of him to claim he has solutions to the country's challenges.

Mudavadi: Ruto is part of Kenya's mess

In the video, the Kenya Kwanza Alliance co-principal added that Kenyans would be foolish to trust Ruto with the country's top job, adding that the politician has been enjoying privileges courtesy of suffering taxpayers.

"These are the people who have ensured Kenya borrowed loans we are now paying as a country. With more debt, the economy is tough. A man can't buy new clothes for his wife because our economy is doing badly. Our sons and brothers have lost jobs and cannot pay rent. How then do you come here to claim you will offer solutions." "If they have been in the government when all these are happening, he can come here to tell you he will change the situation. You are the source of the problem and then you are going to solve it. Nani mjinga? I want to ask you, who is foolish? I have been a vice president and I know when you are the vice or deputy president the government pays all your bills," he added.
