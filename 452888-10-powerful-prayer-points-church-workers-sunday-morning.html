Sunday morning for church workers is not the same as that of regular church members. Several intercessory prayer points need to be made so that the workers will be strengthened and energized before commencing the Sunday service. So what are some of the prayer points for church workers on Sunday morning?

Being a church worker is a blessing from the Lord, but with that blessing comes an extra weight of responsibility that often asks much of a leader. They need to be prayed for to stay on track and not be discouraged. So how do you pray for workers?

Prayer points for church workers on a Sunday morning

Church workers need to be held up in prayer all the time so that they will not fail or falter in their assignments. But how do you lead workers' prayer in the church? Here are some powerful general prayer points that will serve as a guide.

We are thankful that you have granted us the heart and desire for service this morning. Prayer points for ushers in the church

An usher is a spiritual ambassador for the church. The usher serves as the first representative of Jesus Christ in any worship service. As an usher, your spiritual readiness and act of kindness play a huge role in the life-change that takes place that day. Here are some prayer points for ushers that you can make on a Sunday morning.

Almighty God, You are the Alpha and Omega, the Beginning and the End. It's another day you have gathered us here for the Sunday service. As people come to church, we ask that you may help the ushers to be kind to them. Help them give all the congregants a welcome worthy of your Name. Help them display kindness in a way that will prepare the way for those attending the service to encounter Jesus in his word, body and blood. Help every usher to set aside their preoccupations and worries and focus on visitors. We pray this in the precious name of Jesus, Amen.

Dear God, allow kindness to show forth in every usher as they greet each and every person who walks through the door of the church. Allow kindness to help them see any unspoken need and meet it today. Let their ways be pleasing in your sight. May the kindness they share help nervous first-time visitor to feel safe. Let the kindness they show forth help everyone who enters today be at peace. We pray this in the precious name of Jesus, Amen.

Prayer points for church workers and leaders

Believe it or not, church workers and leaders need prayers just like every other individual member of the body of Christ. So you should pray and ask the Lord to anoint them with the spirit of wisdom and leadership as shown.

Heavenly Father, you called unto different assignments in your kingdom, according to the gifts you've bestowed upon them. On this Sunday morning, I pray especially for leaders and workers in the church. I ask that you keep your call fresh in their hearts. Renew and refresh their spirit for the work ahead. May they seek your counsel daily and seek your wisdom and guidance as they give guidance to other church members. Help them always to remember that you are faithful to complete the good work you began in their lives. In Jesus' name, we pray, Amen.

It is also imperative that you pray and ask the Lord to help all the workers to serve faithfully.

Lord Jesus Christ, we ask that you help church leaders be faithful to You and Your church. Help them always to seek Your direction in their life. I pray that they should be faithful in all they do and devoted to their commitments in church. Lord, help them be faithful to proclaim your name in all the earth. As they serve in your house, fill them with grace to do everything faithfully to please You. I praise you for this time that I have to offer up my prayer for faithfulness, Lord and ask that you grant it in the name of Jesus, Amen.

You would agree that the way a church service will go to a great extent depends on worship. And since worship sets the pace for the church service, it is important that those who lead worship be held up in prayer. Here is a sample prayer for a worship team you should pray as you prepare for the Sunday service.
