Manu Chandaria's biography

Manilal Premchand Chandaria, commonly known as Manu Chandaria, is a prominent Kenyan businessman of Indian descent. He was born in Nairobi on March 1, 1929, and is 94 years old (as of 2023). Manilal has seven siblings: three brothers and four sisters. The family resided in the Ngara area of Nairobi.

Manu's parents are Poonjiben Chandaria (mother) and Premchandbhai Chandaria (father). Premchandbhai was an Indian merchant from Saurashtra, Gujarat. He moved to Kenya in 1915 to start a provisions shop along Biashara Street in Nairobi. Growing up, Manu assisted his father in the shop.

Educational background

Manu began school in Nairobi, attending kindergarten at Jain Temple. He attended a local primary school in Nairobi before moving to Mombasa with his father, where he joined the Alidina Visram Primary School. After returning to Nairobi, Manu joined Duke of Gloucester School (now Jamhuri High School).

Manu joined Navanagar High School in Mumbai, India, after the family relocated there due to World War II. He then enrolled at the Jamnagar India University, Bombay, in 1944, graduating with a Bachelor of Science in 1947 or 1949. He then moved to the United States, earning both a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Engineering from the University of Oklahoma.

Professional career

Manu returned to Kenya in 1951 and found that the family business had expanded. Their family had amassed substantial holdings, making their entry into the lucrative steel and aluminium industry possible.

The family was part of Comcraft, a business they acquired with Manilal's future father-in-law. They were among the ten individuals who bought Kenya Aluminium from an Indian merchant in 1929. The enterprise had folded during the depression.

Manu joined the then-remaining asset of the company known as Kaluworks Limited, where he began working as the second plant engineer. The company expanded in the 1950s and 1960s, moving to other countries, including Nigeria, Ethiopia, India, Uganda, Congo, and Zambia.

The Chandaria Foundation

The Chandaria Foundation, established by the family in 1955, is a philanthropic organisation operating in over 11 African countries. It aims to improve the lives of citizens through various initiatives, focusing on education, environment, ecology, youth, healthcare, children with special needs, and youth sports. It also makes substantial donations to schools and assists vulnerable children in slums.
