Big Fat Gypsy lawsuit! Traveller wedding dress designer being sued

April 2024 · 1 minute read

Leanne Phillips claims she was unfairly dismissed from Thelma Madine’s Nico Bridal Company.

She has now set up her own rival company designing dresses for the travelling community.

Leanne, 31, said: “My job at Nico was my life and losing it was heartbreaking. I believe I was unfairly treated after a long and loyal service. It has been really hard to come to terms with.”

The case comes just a year after Thelma, 61, praised Leanne for her loyalty in her autobiography The Gypsy Dressmaker, and said she was taking the company “to another level”

In the book Thel­ma admitted being “a bit of a control freak”.

Mum-of-three Thelma, who started sewing 18 years ago after the breakdown of her marriage, and sold old-fashioned christening gowns on a Liverpool market stall, added: “I know the future of Nico is in Leanne’s hands.

“She reminds me of me at her age – full of imaginative ideas and passion for the task in hand.’’

The tribunal, which takes place next Monday, is expected to centre on the atmosphere at the Liverpool company loved by young traveller brides.

Leanne, who was sacked from her £400-a-week job last Christmas, said: “I cannot wait until this is all over.”

Thelma declined to comment on the case.
