The original Twin Peaks followed an investigation into the murder of popular homecoming queen Laura Palmer in the fictional Washington town of the title.

Headed by FBI Special Agent Dale Cooper, played by Kyle MacLachlan, the investigation took many left-of-centre twists and turns.

Starting out as a crime drama, the narrative incorporated horror, the supernatural and surrealist themes, underpinned by David Lynch's idiosyncratic direction.

Often regarded as one of the greatest television dramas ever made, Twin Peaks ran for two seasons from 1990 to 1991 and was followed with a prequel film in 1992, Fire Walk With Me.
