Britney Spears looks loved-up with her husband Sam Asghari during date night

July 2024 · 5 minute read

Britney Spears looked loved-up with her husband Sam Asghari as they headed out with friends to celebrity hotspot Nobu in Malibu on Sunday night.

The singer, 41, looked effortlessly chic in black jeans and a pink patterned crop top, which she styled with a smart beige jacket.

She completed her look with a pair of black slip-on shoes and kept her essentials in a grey square handbag for the occasion.

Britney shielded her eyes with aviator sunglasses and appeared to be in jovial spirits as she laughed and joked with her model husband, 28.

She styled her golden locks loosely in natural curls cascading across her shoulders as she joined Sam and a group of friends for the lavish meal out.

Sam kept it casual in a grey hoodie and a suave brown blazer and styled his dark locks in a spiked style and sported a well-groomed beard.

The couple's outing comes after Britney posted a Valentine's Day tribute to Sam – one week after slamming claims her loved ones tried to stage an intervention over fears she 'might die'.

She gushed over her husband, who she tied the knot with last June, and shared throwback snaps from their special day as well as pictures of them on holiday.

'TB of Maui magic day!!!' Britney declared in her caption. 'I love you so much baby!!! Your B-day is coming soon!!!'

Sam also posted his own tribute to the songstress, sharing their wedding picture and a collection of sweet snippets.

'Happy Valentine's Day to my valentine,' Sam captioned their black-and-white wedding picture, which showed him and Britney sharing a romantic kiss.

Taking to his Instagram Story, he recorded himself opening a Valentine's Day card from the Hold Me Closer singer.

Sam told Britney 'Thank you baby,' and she cooed back 'You're welcome' as he opened up her card.

Britney's card read: 'You bring out the best in me, and because of you, I feel like anything is possible. I love you. Happy Valentine's Day.'

Penning a handwritten message, Britney added: 'I LOVE YOU HESAM. Love, Britney.'

It came following reports from TMZ that claimed Britney's friends and family staged a failed intervention due to 'concerns she would die' amid alleged mental health struggles. She reportedly sparked concern after she after a 'manic' restaurant episode last month.

It had been reported that sources close to Britney's family claimed they became alarmed by her 'erratic' and 'volatile' behavior due to taking medications that 'hype her up' and cause her to 'fly off the handle'.

However, Britney later lashed out at the claims she might die in an Instagram post, adding she might have to stop posting on social media.

She wrote: 'It makes me sick to my stomach that it's even legal for people to make up stories that I almost died… 

'I mean at some point enough is enough !!! I'm probably going to have to stop posting on Instagram because even though I enjoy doing it, there's obviously a lot of people who don't wish me well !!!'

She went on to say: 'I'm honestly not surprised at all … Again doing the best I can !!! Again, the conservatorship has been over for almost a year … No folks, it's not 2007 … it's 2023 and I'm making my first homemade lasagna at home !!! 

'I finally got my fireplace to work in my living room !!! As my hubby says it best: don't believe everything you read !!! All that love right back at ya !!!' 

In another Instagram post, Britney blasted the reports once again and denied that she has a 'management team' or 'medical doctors'.

Captioning a video message, she wrote: 'This week in the news it said that I almost died and that I have a manager and 'doctors'!!! I don't have a management team nor will I ever again in my life!!! I don't have 'medical doctors'!!!

'I take Prozac for depression and that's about it!!! I'm an extremely boring person!!!' 

Her husband Sam also issued a statement to Access Hollywood denying that any sort of intervention took place.

'An intervention did not occur,' he said. 'My wife is in full control of her life and will continue to make all decisions involving her care regardless of circumstances. Speculation on her health is inappropriate and should end immediately.'
