What is the most successful product on Shark Tank?

February 2024 · 7 minute read

Most people watch ABC’s “Shark Tank” purely for entertainment purposes, but for entrepreneurs, this is where to pitch their businesses and products and get the much-needed capital for taking those businesses off the ground. The ‘Sharks’ can only invest their money if they are convinced that the product or business will be successful. Many businesses have been turned down on this show, and even though some did not become successful as the ‘Sharks’ had predicted, some went on to flourish without investment from this show. The most successful product on Shark Tank has made millions of dollars in profit and made even more entrepreneurs to follow this path.

Shark Tank premiered in 2009, and in the last decade, the show has made a kill out of making businesses. Upcoming entrepreneurs pitch their product ideas to a panel of potential investors made of Dallas Mavericks owner Mark Cuban, Lori Greiner (Queen of QVC), and acerbic Kevin O’Leary. The idea is that if the product is good, it will be encouraged, and investment capital will be provided. On the other hand, questionable inventions are scorned, and owners are sent back to the drawing board.

The most successful product on Shark Tank

Investors are some of the wealthiest people in the world. Therefore, they tend to handpick only the products that are worth their time. Many deals don’t go beyond the due diligence stage, and very few go on to succeed. The following are the most successful products:

1. Bombas

When it comes to the best Shark Tank products, Bombas will always be among the most successful. The owners of Bombas appeared on Shark Tank in season 6 in 2014, seeking a $200,000 capital for which John was given a 17% stake in the business. The enterprise went on to make about $225 million in lifetime sales.

The co-founders of this venture are David Heath and Randy Goldberg, who pride themselves in creating very comfortable socks. Furthermore, for every pair of socks Bombas sold, they donated another pair.

2. Scrub Daddy

Scrub Daddy is a sponge company that has made over $209 million in sales since its launch, becoming one of the most successful Shark Tank products. In 2012, the founder of Scrub Daddy, Aaron Krause, pitched his product for the 4th season of Shark Tank.

He did his research and made sure he was prepared before approaching the Sharks. Greiner offered to invest in the company with $200,000 in exchange for a 25% stake in the company. This turned out to be one of Greiner’s most successful investments in the show.

3. Squatty Potty

Squatty Potty is a unique product that had the approval of the Sharks from the first moment they saw it and is among the best Shark Tank ideas. It is a bathroom footstool invented for creating a healthy toilet posture. Therefore, the product prevents and helps with constipation. Since its launch in 2011, the company has generated close to $164 million in sales, making it one of the most successful Shark Tank products.

4. Simply Fit Board

Simply Fit Board is a new workout tool invented by Linda Clark and her mother, Gloria Hoffman. Yet again, Greiner proved to have an eye for promising products as she offered to invest $125,000 for a 20% stake in the company. 5. The Original Comfy

The Original Comfy is a company that sells a line of luxurious blanket-sweatshirt hybrids. Since its inception, it has accumulated over $150 million in sales, thus making it to feature among Shark Tank success stories.

The pitch for this product was aired in 2017 when the show was in season 9. This was Barbara Corcoran’s favourite pitch, and because of that, she offered $50,000 for a 30% stake in the company.

6. Tipsy Elves

Tipsy Elves is a company that sells ugly Christmas sweaters. In the last nine years since its inception, Tipsy Elves have made $125 million in sales through their unique product which is one of Shark Tank’s biggest successes.

People usually sell nice and beautiful Christmas products, but the Tipsy Elves founders thought that doing the opposite would be a unique business idea, and it ended up paying well. On this product, Herjavec invested his 100,000 for a 10% stake of the company.

7. The Bouqs

In 2014 the founders of The Bouqs pitched their idea of selling bouquet flowers online. This was the 5th season of Shark Tank, and unfortunately, the Bouqs did not get any offer during their presentation. Later on, Herjavec decided to invest in the company three years later.

At that time, Herjavec needed flowers for his wedding, and The Bouqs owners got the chance to explain more about the flower business, and he loved it. Momentarily, the company has made $100 million from sales since its launch.

8. Sleep Styler

The Sleep Styler company specializes in manufacturing heat-free hair rollers. The founders of the company did their pitch on Shark Tank in season 8. Greiner was very interested in this product which was a sign that the potential for its success was very high.

Greiner was positive that in less than a year of the product’s introduction, it would become a household name. As a result, Greiner offered to invest $75,000 for a 25% stake in the company, which is one of the most prudent Shark Tank investments. Today, Sleep Styler has made about $100 million in lifetime sales.

9. Lovepop

In December 2015, Wombi Rose and Wise appeared on Shark Tank seeking capital for Lovepop. Their product was a pop-up greeting card they had developed and intended to supply with help from the Shark Tank.

Kevin O’Leary offered to invest $300,000 in exchange for a 15% stake in the firm. Right from its inception, the enterprise has made about $80 million.

10. Cousins Maine Lobster

This is a seafood truck business. The owners sought capital in season 4 of Shark Tank in 2012. Cousins Maine was co-founded by Sabin Lomac and Jim Tselikis.

Entrepreneur Barbara Corcoran invested $55,000 in the company in exchange for a 15% stake. Their fleet of trucks has grown since then with the company generating $65 million in lifetime sales.

Shark Tank has been around for a decade, and the impact of the show has been felt globally. The sharks do not beat around the bush. If the product does not show promise, they will not invest in it. Thousands of start-ups have given their all to pitch their products before the panel, but only the very best have been chosen to benefit through capital investment. As a result, with thousands of dollars invested, the most successful product on Shark Tank has given a return of millions of dollars.
