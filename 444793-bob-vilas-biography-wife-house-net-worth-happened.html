Bob Vila's biography

Robert Joseph Vila was born on June 20th, 1946, in Miami, Florida, United States. Little information is available about his family background. The identity of his parents and siblings, if any, is not known.

Bob went to Miami Jackson High School and finished his high school studies in 1962. Later on, he joined the University of Florida to pursue a degree in journalism. After graduating, he served in the peace corps from 1971 to 1973.

Career

Vila had an illustrious profession working as a television host. He started his career in 1979 when he was hired to host This Old House. He ran the show with Norm Abram until 1989. They used to renovate old houses.

In 1990, Bob began hosting Bob Vila’s Home Again. The show was renamed Bob Vila in 2005. It aired successfully for 16 seasons on CBS television. During this time, he also appeared in the commercials of Sears.
