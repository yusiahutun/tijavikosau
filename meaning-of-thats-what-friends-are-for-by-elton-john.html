Meaning of Thats What Friends Are For by Elton John

March 2024 · 4 minute read

In the enchanting world of music, there are songs that touch our hearts, make us reflect on life, and even celebrate the unbreakable bond of friendship. Elton John's timeless classic, "That's What Friends Are For," undeniably falls into this latter category. The song's overall theme revolves around the power of friendship, emphasizing the unconditional support, comfort, and joy it brings into our lives.One standout lyric from the song that beautifully encapsulates this theme is: "Knowing you can always count on me, for sure." This line speaks to the unwavering reliability and trustworthiness that exists within true friendships. It reminds us that no matter the circumstances, we will always have someone we can rely on, someone who will offer a helping hand or a listening ear.On a deeper level, this lyric could also represent the idea of stability and constancy. True friends are like beacons of light in our lives, providing a sense of security and reassurance. They remain steadfast, even when faced with life's ups and downs. In a world that can often feel unpredictable, the presence of a loyal friend can bring us solace and grounding.Another significant lyric from the song is: "And I never thought I'd feel this way." While seemingly straightforward at first glance, this line holds a profound message about the transformative nature of friendship. It suggests that friendship has the power to change us, to make us see the world in a new light, and to awaken emotions we never thought possible.This lyric explores the theme of personal growth and self-discovery through friendship. True friends not only support us during challenging times but also inspire us to become better versions of ourselves. They encourage us to step outside our comfort zones, pursue new experiences, and embrace the unfamiliar. Ultimately, they expand our horizons and help us realize our full potential.Building upon the idea of personal growth, let's delve into the lyric: "And so by the way, I thank you." This expression of gratitude highlights the theme of appreciation in friendship. True friends are there for us through thick and thin, offering a shoulder to cry on, lending an ear to listen, and providing wise counsel when needed. Expressing gratitude is not only a testament to the value we place on our friendships but also a reminder to cherish those who enrich our lives.Moreover, this lyric can also speak to the power of reciprocity in friendships. While we may be the ones thanking our friends in this instance, it is also essential to acknowledge that they too benefit from our presence in their lives. The give-and-take nature of friendship fosters a sense of balance and mutual support, making the bond even stronger.Moving on to another standout lyric, we encounter the thought-provoking line, "And then if you can remember." This phrase sparks the theme of nostalgia and reminiscence. As time passes, friendships evolve, and memories are made. This lyric reminds us of the importance of cherishing and preserving these memories.Friendship creates a tapestry of shared experiences, inside jokes, and heartfelt moments. It is within these memories that the true essence of friendship resides. In reminiscing, we are reminded of the laughter, the tears, and the special connections we have formed. Nostalgia strengthens bonds, reminding us of the deep-rooted history we share with our friends.Additionally, "That's What Friends Are For" delves into the concept of unity and solidarity through the standout lyric: "Let's all swear that if It isn’t dead, It ever comes back, we will too!" This line evokes a sense of camaraderie and loyalty that transcends time and distance. It symbolizes the unbreakable bond among friends, promising unwavering support, no matter the circumstances.This lyric speaks to the theme of resilience and collective strength within friendships. When faced with adversity, friends stand by one another, determined to weather any storm that comes their way. It emphasizes that true friends will always have our backs, remaining by our side through thick and thin.In conclusion, Elton John's "That's What Friends Are For" explores the profound and enduring nature of friendship. Through its standout lyrics, the song touches upon themes of reliability, personal growth, appreciation, nostalgia, and unity. It reminds us of the invaluable role that friends play in our lives, bringing joy, comfort, and unwavering support. As we navigate the ebbs and flows of life, it is our friends who provide the strength and love that help us stay afloat. So, let's celebrate these extraordinary connections, for they are what make life all the more beautiful.
